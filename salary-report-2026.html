The 2026 Remote Staffing Salary Report

US, Canada, and India compensation data across 8 role categories. Cost-savings analysis, hiring trends, and what it all means for small and mid-sized businesses.

In 2026, the cost gap between US/Canadian and Indian professionals continues to widen. This report compiles publicly available salary data from government and industry sources to help business owners make informed staffing decisions.

Key findings

• A typical US full-stack developer now costs 4-5x more annually than an equivalent managed remote developer from India.

• Benefits and overhead add 30-40% on top of US base salaries, often overlooked in cost comparisons.

• Time-to-hire for US-based roles averages 42 days; managed remote staffing can reduce this to 7 days.

• The 8 role categories analyzed show 60-70% cost savings when hiring via managed remote staffing from India.

Methodology

This report aggregates publicly available compensation data from the following sources:

  • US data: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS.gov Occupational Employment Statistics), Glassdoor, Indeed Salary Data
  • Canada data: Statistics Canada, Glassdoor Canada, Indeed Canada
  • India data: Glassdoor India, Naukri.com, AmbitionBox, PayScale India
  • Teckas pricing: Our published managed staffing rates (all-inclusive monthly fees)

Figures reflect typical mid-market salaries for professionals with 2-7 years of experience. Actual compensation varies by city, industry, and seniority. We cite ranges rather than single data points to reflect real-world variance.

Part 5: Hidden Costs That Change the Calculation

The salary comparison alone under-states the full picture. Hiring a local employee in the US or Canada involves costs that are easy to miss:

  • Recruitment and ad spend: Industry averages put US cost-per-hire at $4,000-$5,000 including job boards, recruiter fees, screening time, and interview hours.
  • Benefits: Healthcare, dental, 401(k) match, PTO, payroll taxes, workers' comp. Adds 25-40% to base salary depending on region.
  • Onboarding and ramp time: Productive output usually starts 60-90 days after day one. Full ramp to peak productivity can take 6 months.
  • Turnover risk: BLS reports average US employee tenure is about 4 years overall but under 2 years in many service roles. Replacing costs add up.
  • Management overhead: Local hires require more hours of direct supervision, HR admin, and performance management.

Part 7: 2026 Market Trends

US Salary Pressure Continues

BLS 2026 data shows wages in professional and technical categories are rising 3-4% year-over-year, outpacing general inflation. Competition for skilled labor in tech hubs is particularly intense.

Remote Work Normalization

Stanford research and multiple industry surveys in 2024-2025 confirmed that hybrid and fully remote work arrangements are now mainstream. Small and mid-sized businesses that resisted remote models have largely capitulated, accepting remote hires alongside in-office.

India's English-Speaking Talent Pool Expanding

India graduates approximately 2.5 million English-speaking college graduates annually (various public sources). Salary arbitrage remains substantial but has narrowed in tech roles, where India's top-tier developers can command $40,000-$60,000/year. For mid-market businesses, the mid-tier India market remains deeply cost-advantaged.

Managed Staffing Replacing DIY Outsourcing

Direct-to-freelancer platforms (Upwork, Fiverr) remain large but are losing share in long-term hires to managed providers offering full-time placements with compliance, payroll, and replacement guarantees.

Part 8: What This Means for Your Business

If you're a US or Canadian business owner evaluating remote staffing in 2026, three practical takeaways:

1. The total-cost gap is larger than most realize

Comparing "monthly fee" to "annual salary" is misleading. Always compare total loaded cost: salary + benefits + recruitment + management overhead. The 60-75% savings figure holds consistently when done correctly.

2. Manager time is the hidden constraint

The reason managed staffing works for busy SMB owners: the provider absorbs HR, payroll, IT, and operational overhead. You get output, not management burden.

3. Time zone alignment matters more than cost

A remote hire that doesn't work your hours is worth less than a local hire. Ensure your provider guarantees working-hour alignment, not just access. Teckas remote staff work client business hours; this is non-negotiable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these salary figures accurate for specific US cities?

The figures represent national medians. Cost of living adjustments apply: San Francisco, New York, Boston, and Seattle typically run 20-40% higher. Texas and Florida tend to run slightly below national median in some roles. For Canadian data, Toronto and Vancouver approach US East Coast levels.

How often is this data updated?

This report is refreshed annually. Underlying sources (BLS Occupational Employment Statistics, Glassdoor, Statistics Canada) publish on various cadences. We recommend checking BLS and Glassdoor directly for role-specific spot checks.

Do India salaries include benefits?

Teckas rates are all-inclusive: the monthly fee covers the professional's salary, benefits (Provident Fund, ESI, health insurance), payroll processing, IT equipment, internet, and Teckas account management. There are no additional fees, no setup costs, and no hidden charges.

Can I cite this report?

Yes. Please cite as "2026 Remote Staffing Salary Report, Teckas Technologies, teckas.com/salary-report-2026.html." Reproducing full tables with attribution is permitted.

How do I evaluate if remote staffing fits my business?

Three questions to answer honestly: (1) Is the role fully or mostly remote-executable? (2) Can the tasks be clearly defined and reviewed? (3) Am I paying a US/Canada premium for work that a well-trained remote professional could do equally well? If yes to all three, remote staffing is a strong candidate for cost savings.
