Why Ohio Businesses Are Going Remote

Ohio is an SMB and mid-market state. The Ohio Chamber of Commerce reports that 99.6 percent of Ohio's 950,000 businesses are classified as small. Those owners are doing what SMB owners everywhere do: running sales, ops, dispatch, accounting, and customer service personally because the math of a full-time local hire does not work for them. Ohio's working-age population has been flat for a decade, and the Fed's Cleveland District has consistently reported tight labor in skilled trades, nursing, and logistics.

At the same time, Ohio has real industrial density. Columbus is a top 15 US metro and a logistics hub (Intel's new fab, Amazon air freight at Wilmington, major 3PL operators). Cleveland has a deep healthcare cluster around Cleveland Clinic and University Hospitals. Cincinnati has a strong CPG and P&G-adjacent ecosystem. The common thread is mid-sized operators who need back-office leverage without committing to a new $60K+ loaded hire.

How Working in Ohio Eastern Time Works

Ohio runs on Eastern Time year-round. India is 9.5 to 10.5 hours ahead. A 9am to 6pm ET shift maps to 6:30pm to 3:30am IST, which is a standard full-time shift we hire for. Your VA or bookkeeper is live when you are, not 12 hours behind.
