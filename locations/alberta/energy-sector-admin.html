Why Alberta Energy Operators Hire Remote Admins

Alberta produces the overwhelming majority of Canada's crude oil and natural gas per the Canada Energy Regulator (CER) and Alberta Energy Regulator (AER) data. Calgary anchors the E&P and midstream industry with Canadian Natural Resources, Suncor, Cenovus, Imperial Oil, Tourmaline, ARC Resources, and hundreds of independent operators and service companies. Behind the rig counts sits a huge back-office stack: joint venture billing, AFE management, Petrinex volumetric and royalty reporting, AER submissions via OneStop, and Crown and freehold royalty calculations. The specialization required (Qbyte FM, Qbyte Metrix, Pandell JV, Enverus Canada) keeps local labor pricing elevated.

Calgary energy admin and JV accountant pay per Robert Half Canada runs CAD $65,000 to $88,000 per year, or CAD $7,500 to $10,000 loaded, with senior JV and royalty analysts clearing CAD $120,000. The cyclical nature of the Alberta labor market means operators often struggle to staff up during drilling pushes and are overstaffed during downturns. Managed remote admin capacity solves that flex problem.

Statistics Canada NOC 12110 (administrative officers) and NOC 11100 (financial auditors and accountants) data tracks Calgary energy sector pay at a premium to national medians.
