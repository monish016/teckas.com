Why Florida Businesses Are Going Remote

Florida has been the fastest-growing state in the US by net migration since 2020, according to US Census data, adding hundreds of thousands of residents per year to metros like Tampa, Orlando, and Jacksonville. That growth has outpaced the labor pool in exactly the industries that service new residents: HVAC and plumbing, real estate brokerages, property managers, hospitality and short-term rentals, and home builders. The Florida Chamber of Commerce has flagged a persistent worker gap across service industries, and labor-force participation has not kept up with demand.

Florida also has no state income tax, which has drawn a wave of private-equity-backed home services rollups and finance firms relocating from New York and New Jersey. That capital is buying up $5M to $50M home services operators and consolidating them, and those operators are the ones calling Teckas to staff dispatch desks, CSR teams, and back-office billing at a cost that actually makes the unit economics work.

How Working in Florida Eastern Time Works

Florida runs on Eastern Time. India is 9.5 to 10.5 hours ahead. A standard 8am to 5pm ET Florida shift becomes 5:30pm to 2:30am IST. We hire dedicated staff for that shift so your CSR, dispatcher, or coordinator is live during your Florida business hours, not at the tail end of their day. For hurricane-season overflow or extended summer HVAC hours, we can layer a second shift to cover into the evening.
