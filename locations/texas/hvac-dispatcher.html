Why Texas HVAC Shops Hire Remote Dispatchers

Texas runs one of the largest residential HVAC markets in the country. The Texas Workforce Commission counts more than 30,000 licensed air conditioning technicians across the state, and the Houston metro alone is home to several thousand licensed HVAC contractors serving a population of over 7 million. Summer cooling season in Houston and Dallas pushes average daytime highs into the triple digits for weeks at a time, and larger shops routinely see inbound call volume triple between May and September. A missed no-cool call in a Texas July is not a small problem. It is a lost $400 to $1,500 service ticket and a customer who may not call back.

At the same time, local HVAC dispatcher wages in Texas have climbed. Indeed lists Houston and Dallas HVAC dispatcher pay at $22 to $28 per hour, and Glassdoor shows a Texas average of roughly $51,000 per year before benefits and payroll tax. Loaded out, a local seat runs $4,200 to $5,800 per month. For a 6 to 20 truck shop fighting margin pressure from rising equipment costs, that is real money. A remote dispatcher working your Central Time shift for a third of the cost lets you keep the phones answered without another desk in the office.

The Bureau of Labor Statistics reports the median annual wage for dispatchers (except police, fire, and ambulance) at $46,670 as of May 2023. Texas urban metros trend above that median, particularly in home services, which is exactly where remote staffing economics make the strongest case.
