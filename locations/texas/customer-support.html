Why Texas Retail and Ecommerce Brands Hire Remote CS

Texas has quietly become one of the most concentrated DTC and retail markets in the US. Austin anchors a growing DTC cluster with brands like YETI, Kendra Scott, and Outdoor Voices scaling out of the city, and the South Austin warehouse belt along 183 and Ben White is packed with 3PLs handling Shopify-based brands. Dallas anchors the traditional retail side, home to AT&T, JC Penney, and the country's largest retail convention floor at the Dallas Market Center. Houston rounds out the picture with a heavy ecommerce logistics footprint tied to the Port of Houston.

Growing DTC brands in Austin and Dallas all hit the same staffing wall around 200 to 500 tickets per day. Hiring local CSRs at $22 to $26 an hour eats into gross margin fast, and the local talent pool for experienced Zendesk and Gorgias agents is thin. The BLS May 2023 report puts national median customer service representative pay at $39,680 per year, but Austin and Dallas job postings routinely clear $45,000 to $52,000 with benefits. Loaded, that is $3,800 to $5,200 per month per seat.

A Teckas remote customer support agent on Central Time covers your full business day, handles tickets in your tone of voice, and costs a third of the local seat.
