Why Texas Businesses Hire Remote Bookkeepers

Texas is unusual among large state economies. It has no personal or corporate income tax, which simplifies one layer of bookkeeping, but it still has a Texas franchise tax (the "margin tax") administered by the Comptroller of Public Accounts and an active sales and use tax regime that reaches into most service industries. For operators in Houston, Dallas, Austin, and San Antonio, the bookkeeping work is less about state income tax and more about clean AR and AP, accurate sales tax by jurisdiction, and audit-ready franchise tax workpapers.

The industries that dominate Texas payrolls also carry specific accounting quirks. Oil and gas operators in the Permian Basin and Eagle Ford run joint interest billing, revenue distribution, AFE tracking, and severance tax workpapers. Home services companies across Texas track trust accounting for deposits, warranty reserves, and technician commissions. Austin SaaS companies manage deferred revenue, MRR waterfalls, and Stripe reconciliations. A good bookkeeper who understands one or more of these lanes is expensive to hire locally.

BLS May 2023 data reports median pay for bookkeeping, accounting, and auditing clerks at $47,440 per year nationally. Houston and Dallas trend 10 to 20 percent above that median, and once benefits and payroll taxes are loaded in, a Texas bookkeeper costs $4,800 to $6,500 per month. A managed remote bookkeeper through Teckas starts at $1,500 per month and handles the same day-to-day reconciliation, categorization, and reporting work.
