Why Illinois Businesses Are Going Remote

Illinois is a structurally tight labor market. Since 2020 the state has been net-negative on migration, with working-age residents leaving faster than they arrive, per US Census ACS data. The effect is most visible in Chicago, where the Fed's Beige Book has repeatedly flagged difficulty filling operational, analytical, and technical roles even with wage increases. Chicago is the country's third-largest financial services hub (CME, derivatives, insurance), a massive logistics node, and a healthcare employer cluster. All of those sectors depend on coordinator, analyst, and back-office roles that are now hard to staff and expensive to retain.

Illinois also has a high combined state and local tax burden and commercial rent per seat in the Loop remains elevated. The combination pushes headcount math for any role that does not have to be physically present in Chicago. Teckas is used by Chicago finance ops teams, insurance adjusters, logistics brokers, and manufacturing SMBs to extend team capacity on Central Time without growing Illinois headcount.

How Working in Illinois Central Time Works

Illinois runs on Central Time. India is 10.5 to 11.5 hours ahead. A Chicago 8am to 5pm CT shift maps to 6:30pm to 3:30am IST in India. We hire teammates specifically for that window. For logistics and freight brokerage clients that need early-morning carrier contact, we can also run a pre-market shift overlapping Chicago's 5am to 2pm CT window.

Compliance and Employment Notes

Illinois has a growing stack of employment laws (One Day Rest in Seven Act, Paid Leave for All Workers Act, BIPA) but all of them apply to Illinois-based workers. Teckas staff are full-time employees of Teckas Technologies Pvt. Ltd. in India, so Illinois wage, hour, and leave laws do not apply to the engagement. You pay a monthly service fee, not wages. For finance and insurance clients we can sign NDAs, DPAs, and SOC 2 aligned data handling commitments.
