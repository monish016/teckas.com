Why Illinois Businesses Hire Remote Bookkeepers

Illinois sits at the center of Midwest commerce with a diversified economy: freight and logistics in Chicago, manufacturing in Rockford and Peoria, agribusiness across central Illinois, and a huge concentration of financial services, law, and professional firms in the Loop. For any business doing more than $500,000 in revenue, keeping books clean under Illinois' Retailers' Occupation Tax (ROT) structure is non-trivial. Illinois has a 6.25 percent state sales tax base plus home-rule municipal and county add-ons. Chicago alone stacks to 10.25 percent. Multi-location retailers and service providers have dozens of jurisdictions to reconcile.

Local Chicago bookkeepers run $52,000 to $68,000 per year per Indeed and Glassdoor, with senior bookkeepers and staff accountants clearing $75,000. Loaded, $5,000 to $6,500 per month. Illinois also carries higher employment overhead than neighboring states (Illinois individual income tax is 4.95 percent flat, plus unemployment insurance that runs higher than many states), which pushes real cost higher.

A Teckas bookkeeper works your Central Time hours in your QuickBooks, Xero, or NetSuite, handles multi-jurisdiction sales tax with Avalara or TaxJar, and prepares workpapers for your CPA. BLS May 2023 national median for bookkeeping clerks is $47,440; Illinois metros trend 10 to 20 percent above.
