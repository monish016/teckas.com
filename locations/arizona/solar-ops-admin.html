Why Arizona Solar Installers Hire Remote Ops Admins

Arizona is one of the top solar states in the country. The Solar Energy Industries Association (SEIA) consistently ranks Arizona in the top five for installed solar capacity per capita, and the Phoenix metro is home to hundreds of residential and commercial installers. Behind every install is a paper chain that can make or break a project's margin: HOA architectural approvals, AHJ permits in Maricopa County, Pima County, and dozens of city jurisdictions, APS, SRP, TEP, and UNS Electric interconnection applications, and lender stipulation follow-up with Sunlight Financial, GoodLeap, Mosaic, and Dividend.

Local Phoenix solar ops coordinator pay per Indeed runs $48,000 to $62,000 per year loaded. High-volume installers often need 3 to 6 admins on a 200-install-per-month pipeline, and local hiring cannot keep up with the growth cycle. Turnover is meaningful, and a gap in interconnection processing can push install dates back by weeks.

A Teckas solar ops admin works your Mountain Time hours and owns your permitting and interconnection queue inside Solo, Aurora, OpenSolar, or Scoop.
