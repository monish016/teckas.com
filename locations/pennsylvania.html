Why Pennsylvania Businesses Are Going Remote

Pennsylvania has two distinct economic engines. Philadelphia is a major East Coast healthcare, pharma, and education hub (Penn Medicine, Jefferson, CHOP, Comcast, Independence Blue Cross). Pittsburgh is a rebuilt tech and robotics economy (Carnegie Mellon, Google Pittsburgh, Aurora, Duolingo) anchored on UPMC, the largest employer in the state. In between, the Lehigh Valley and Harrisburg corridor host dense industrial, logistics, and manufacturing operations. PA's working-age population has been flat since 2010 per US Census data, which has left most of these employers structurally short-staffed at the coordinator and back-office level.

Healthcare is the most acute pressure point. PA ranks near the top nationally for nursing and medical admin vacancies (per PA Department of Labor data), and private practices cannot compete with UPMC / Penn salary bands. That has pushed a wave of PA medical groups toward remote billing and scheduling. Manufacturing operators running order entry, AR/AP, and customer service face the same gap. Teckas is used across these segments to add Eastern-Time capacity without expanding PA headcount.
