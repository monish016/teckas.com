Why California Agencies Hire Remote Marketers

Los Angeles and the Bay Area together host the densest concentration of independent digital agencies in North America. Between Santa Monica, Culver City, Venice, and SF's SoMa and FiDi, thousands of performance, SEO, and lifecycle shops compete for the same mid-level marketers. AdAge and Built In LA both track the problem: LA digital marketing specialist salaries now routinely run $65,000 to $90,000 per year for 2 to 4 years of experience, and senior paid media buyers clear six figures. Glassdoor reports similar numbers in San Francisco.

That wage pressure meets an agency business model that rarely bills above $150 per hour for a mid-level specialist. Margins are thin. Layer on California's employment complexity, AB5, CalSavers, mandatory sick leave, SB 553 workplace violence plans, and the all-in cost of a local marketer routinely hits $80,000 to $110,000 per year. Agencies trying to scale from 5 to 20 retainer clients without doubling overhead have quietly shifted execution to managed remote staff.

Teckas places full-time remote digital marketers for California agencies at $1,500 to $2,800 per month. They work Pacific Time overlap, run your ad accounts inside your Google Ads MCC and Meta Business Manager, and report the same way an in-house hire would. BLS May 2023 lists national median marketing specialist pay at $74,680, with California metros running 20 to 40 percent above.
