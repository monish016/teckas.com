Why California Startups Hire Remote Developers

California is still the world's largest concentration of venture capital and tech talent, but the cost side has become brutal. Levels.fyi data shows mid-level software engineer base pay in the San Francisco Bay Area running $170,000 to $210,000, with total comp routinely crossing $280,000 once equity and bonus are included. Los Angeles tech has followed the same curve, with Santa Monica and Culver City postings for senior engineers now clearing $200,000 base. Built In SF and Built In LA both regularly report dev seats taking 8 to 14 weeks to fill.

Add California's specific employment overhead, state income tax up to 13.3 percent, SB 553 workplace violence plan requirements, AB5 contractor classification risk, and mandatory CalSavers or employer 401k, and every local hire carries material compliance load. For a seed or Series A startup still trying to hit the next milestone, burning $20,000+ per month on a single engineer is not sustainable.

A Teckas remote developer is a full-time Teckas employee in India working your Pacific Time overlap window, shipping inside your GitHub and Jira, at $2,800 to $4,500 per month all-in. No AB5, no California W-2, no benefits administration on your side. The BLS May 2023 national median for software developers is $132,270, and California metros run 40 to 70 percent above that median. The savings are not marginal.
