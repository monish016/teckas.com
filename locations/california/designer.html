Why California Brands Hire Remote Designers

California is the design capital of North America. Los Angeles anchors entertainment and film, with Netflix, Disney, Paramount, and thousands of production shops driving demand for key art, motion, and title design. San Francisco anchors product design, with Figma itself, Airbnb, Stripe, Notion, and Linear all headquartered in the Bay Area. San Diego's biotech and mobility cluster plus LA's exploding DTC brand scene round out the picture. That concentration makes California the best place in the country to work with designers and the most expensive place in the country to hire them.

Glassdoor data puts senior product designer base pay in SF at $150,000 to $195,000, and senior brand designer pay in LA at $110,000 to $145,000. Built In LA lists motion designer salaries at $95,000 to $125,000. Levels.fyi shows Stripe and Airbnb product designers above $220,000 total comp. When a $50 million DTC brand just needs someone to ship three Instagram ad variants and two landing pages a week, hiring a $150,000 LA senior full-time is not proportional to the work.

A Teckas remote designer works your Pacific Time overlap inside your Figma team, ships on your sprint cadence, and costs $1,800 to $3,500 per month. BLS May 2023 lists the national median for graphic designers at $57,990 and for web and digital interface designers at $83,240, with California metros trending 40 to 80 percent above.
