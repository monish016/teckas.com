Why Georgia Logistics Firms Hire Remote Coordinators

Georgia is one of the most important logistics states in the US. Atlanta's Hartsfield-Jackson is the world's busiest passenger airport and a major air cargo node, with Delta Cargo and UPS Worldport (in Louisville) feeding through. The Port of Savannah's Garden City Terminal ranks in the top four US container ports per AAPA data, and GPA-run inland rail ramps push Savannah freight directly to Atlanta and beyond. Atlanta itself is the Southeast trucking hub with a dense concentration of freight brokerages, 3PLs, and asset carriers. Behind all of that is a mountain of rate confirmations, BOLs, port appointments, chassis pulls, and demurrage claims.

Atlanta logistics coordinator pay per Indeed runs $50,000 to $65,000 per year, or $4,400 to $5,500 loaded. Broker industry turnover is high per FreightWaves coverage, which means Atlanta shops often need to backfill 2 to 5 seats per quarter. A Teckas coordinator on Eastern Time provides stable execution capacity in McLeod, TMW, Aljex, or CargoWise.

BLS May 2023 median for logisticians is $79,400.
