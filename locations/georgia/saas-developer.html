Why Georgia SaaS and Fintech Hire Remote Developers

Atlanta's tech economy has quietly grown into one of the strongest in the Southeast. The city is the global HQ of NCR, Global Payments, Fiserv's legacy processing arm, and the Transaction Alley cluster that handles roughly 70 percent of US payment volume per the Metro Atlanta Chamber. Salesforce's Atlanta office, Mailchimp (now Intuit), Calendly, Pindrop, OneTrust, and FullStory all anchor a deep SaaS bench. Built In Atlanta and Technology Association of Georgia (TAG) data consistently rank Atlanta in the top 10 US tech hubs.

The wage side has caught up with the talent side. Built In Atlanta reports mid-level software engineer base pay at $115,000 to $145,000, and senior engineers regularly clear $165,000. Loaded with benefits, 401k match, and equity, that is $11,000 to $14,500 per month per seat. Series A and Series B Atlanta SaaS companies increasingly fill execution-level engineering capacity with managed remote staff to stretch runway.

A Teckas developer is a full-time India employee working Eastern Time overlap with your Atlanta team. BLS May 2023 national median for software developers is $132,270, with Atlanta metros running 10 to 20 percent above.
