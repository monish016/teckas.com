Why RTP Biotech Companies Hire Remote QA Associates

Research Triangle Park (RTP) between Raleigh, Durham, and Chapel Hill is one of the largest biotech clusters in North America. Per the NC Biotechnology Center, North Carolina hosts 800+ life sciences companies with a strong GMP manufacturing base. Biogen, Fujifilm Diosynth, Pfizer, Grifols, Eli Lilly, Seqirus, and dozens of emerging biotechs operate facilities in RTP and the surrounding area. Every one of them runs a QA organization that processes deviations, batch record reviews, CAPAs, change controls, and training documentation inside an eQMS like Veeva Vault QMS or MasterControl.

Per Glassdoor and Built In RDU, RTP QA associate pay runs $68,000 to $88,000 per year, and senior QA specialists clear $100,000. Loaded, $6,500 to $8,500 per month. For a Series B biotech trying to buildout a QA function pre-FDA BLA, that headcount burns fast.

A Teckas QA associate works your Eastern Time hours, inside your eQMS, under SOPs you provide. They do documentation-heavy work (not decisioning that requires local licensure) that frees up your senior QA team for audits and approvals. BLS May 2023 median for quality control inspectors is $44,520; biotech QA pay sits well above that baseline.
