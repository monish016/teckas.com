Why Texas Businesses Are Going Remote

Texas is the second-largest state economy in the US and, despite the absence of a state income tax, it is not the low-cost labor market it used to be. The Austin tech corridor has seen engineer and product salaries climb within striking distance of Silicon Valley, with the Austin Chamber and Built In Austin reporting mid-level software salaries routinely above $120,000. Houston is a global energy hub with six-figure pricing even for operations and logistics support roles. Dallas-Fort Worth, now the fourth-largest metro in the country, is fighting a well-documented skilled-worker shortage across finance, healthcare, and home services.

At the same time, Texas has one of the highest concentrations of HVAC, plumbing, and electrical contractors in the country, thanks to year-round construction and brutal summer cooling demand. These are the exact operators who feel payroll pressure first. Remote staffing in Texas gives Houston dispatch desks, Dallas real estate teams, Austin SaaS startups, and San Antonio medical practices a way to scale headcount without hitting local wage ceilings.

How Working in Texas Central Time Works

Texas runs almost entirely on Central Time. India is 10 hours 30 minutes ahead of CT in standard time and 10 hours 30 minutes when Texas is on DST. Our staff work your full business day, not a few hours of overlap. A Houston 8am to 5pm CT shift becomes a 6:30pm to 3:30am IST shift in India, and we hire specifically for that window. You get a teammate who is online when your Texas customers and technicians are, not a contractor catching up on Slack the next morning.

Compliance and Employment Notes

Texas has no state income tax and no state-level restrictions like California AB5, which makes it one of the simpler states for running a remote-augmented team. Your Teckas staff are full-time employees of Teckas Technologies Pvt. Ltd. in India. We handle Indian payroll, statutory benefits (PF, ESI), equipment, and HR. You do not run payroll for them, do not issue a W-2 or 1099, and do not manage Texas Workforce Commission filings on them. That makes the engagement simpler than using a US contractor.
