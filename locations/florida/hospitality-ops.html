Why Florida Hospitality Operators Hire Remote Ops Admins

Florida has roughly 1.3 million licensed lodging units per the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation, from Orlando vacation homes to beachfront condo-hotels in the Panhandle and boutique resorts in the Keys. AirDNA tracks Florida as the second-largest short-term rental market in the US, behind only California. Behind the front desk and the guest experience is a back-office that never really sleeps. Channel manager rate updates, OTA listing photo and content refreshes, tourist tax filings prep, license renewals, and vendor invoice processing all pile up.

Local hospitality ops coordinators in Orlando, Miami, and Tampa run $48,000 to $62,000 loaded per Indeed and Glassdoor data. Florida's seasonal hiring pattern makes retention hard. Admins leave for hotel front desk supervisor roles during season and come back during shoulder months. For operators running 50 to 500 units, that churn is expensive.

A Teckas hospitality ops admin works your Eastern Time hours, handles OTA and channel manager updates daily, and keeps your compliance file current. BLS May 2023 puts first-line supervisors of office and administrative support at $60,890 median, with hospitality-specific roles trending near that figure.
