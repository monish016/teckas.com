Why Georgia Businesses Are Going Remote

Atlanta has quietly become the Southeast's largest tech and SaaS hub. Metro Atlanta is home to Mailchimp (now Intuit), Calendly, Salesloft, Greenlight, SalesLoft, and a heavy fintech cluster (NCR, Global Payments, FIS). That density has bid up compensation fast: Built In Atlanta and Glassdoor regularly quote mid-level engineer salaries between $130K and $160K, with SDRs and customer success roles in the $80K to $110K range. Layer on Atlanta's logistics dominance (Hartsfield-Jackson, UPS, Delta), the Georgia film tax credit that made Atlanta the third-largest film production hub in the US, and you get a state where hiring demand keeps outrunning the local labor pool.

For venture-backed SaaS operators and logistics SMBs, that math breaks the unit economics of a 20 to 50 person team. Teckas is used by Atlanta SaaS companies to staff developers, SDRs, and customer ops on Eastern Time without paying Atlanta comp. Logistics operators around Atlanta use us for carrier and documentation support. Film production coordinators use remote production VAs to handle scheduling and paperwork between shoots.

How Working in Georgia Eastern Time Works

Georgia runs on Eastern Time year-round. India is 9.5 to 10.5 hours ahead. A 9am to 6pm ET Atlanta workday becomes 6:30pm to 3:30am IST, which we staff as a full-time shift.

Compliance and Employment Notes

Georgia is a business-friendly employment state and does not have California or NY-style contractor laws. Teckas staff are employees of Teckas Technologies Pvt. Ltd. in India. You pay a monthly service fee with no Georgia Department of Revenue filings. For fintech clients we sign DPAs and SOC 2 aligned data handling commitments on request.
