Why NYC Businesses Hire Remote

New York City has the most expensive cost of doing business in the United States, and it is not close. Between federal payroll taxes, New York State income tax withholding, New York State unemployment insurance, disability (NYSDI), paid family leave, NYC-specific workers' comp rates, commercial rent in Manhattan and Brooklyn, and the operational overhead that comes with running a team in the five boroughs, a $70,000 base hire commonly lands at $95,000-$105,000 loaded on a real-cost accounting basis. For a SoHo DTC brand, a Flatiron SaaS startup, a Midtown agency, or a Chelsea production studio, that math breaks hard at scale.

Layer on the reality that NYC's talent market is still the most competitive in the country. Finance, media, advertising, fashion, and fintech all draw from overlapping pools of operations and support talent, which means NYC SMBs are routinely bidding against Goldman Sachs, Condé Nast, WPP, and Stripe for the same operations associate or coordinator. Remote staffing in New York City lets you step out of that auction entirely.

Teckas places full-time, trained remote staff in Eastern Time - aligned perfectly with Manhattan business hours - at one-fourth to one-fifth of the loaded NYC rate. Your senior strategists, creatives, and dealmakers stay in NYC. The operational layer runs through Teckas.

How NYC's Time Zone Works With India

New York City runs on Eastern Time. India Standard Time is 9 hours 30 minutes ahead of ET. A standard NYC 9am to 6pm ET workday maps to 6:30pm to 3:30am IST in India. Our NYC-aligned staff are hired specifically for that shift as their full-time hours - not stretched overlap. Your Midtown team can Slack at 11am and get a reply in 90 seconds.
