Why Austin Businesses Hire Remote

Austin has quietly become the most expensive tech labor market outside the Bay Area and New York. Oracle, Tesla, Apple, Dell, Google, Meta, and Indeed all operate significant engineering campuses here, and the pandemic-era migration of SF founders to Austin pushed startup compensation into territory that mid-stage companies struggle to sustain. Per the Austin Chamber and Built In Austin 2024-2025 salary benchmarks, a mid-level software engineer in Austin now commands roughly $125,000-$150,000 base, with senior engineers often quoted $170,000+ before equity.

For a Series A startup with 18-24 months of runway, that math breaks fast. Founders are forced into painful tradeoffs: hire fewer engineers, delay product bets, or raise dilutive bridge capital. Austin remote staffing through Teckas gives founders a third option - keep your senior architects and PMs local, then build out the pod with trained remote developers, QA engineers, and customer success reps at $1,800-$3,500 per month, all-in, full-time.

Austin also has a distinct cluster of creator-economy, devtools, and fintech startups that benefit from remote customer success and ops support. Our CS reps run Intercom, Zendesk, and HubSpot Service Hub in your business hours.

  • Software developers (frontend, backend, full-stack). React, Node, Python, Ruby, and Go engineers for Austin SaaS and devtools. They ship to your GitHub, join your standups, and work in your sprint cadence.

How Austin's Time Zone Works With India

Austin sits on Central Time. India Standard Time is 10 hours 30 minutes ahead. An Austin startup running a 9am to 6pm CT workday corresponds to 7:30pm to 4:30am IST in India. Our engineers and CS reps work that window as their primary shift, not as a stretched overlap. You get real-time Slack responses during your Austin standup at 10am, not next-day catchup.
