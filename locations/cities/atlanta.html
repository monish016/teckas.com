Why Atlanta Businesses Hire Remote

Atlanta carries more Fortune 500 headquarters per capita than almost any US metro. Coca-Cola, Delta Air Lines, Home Depot, UPS, Southern Company, Truist, Aflac, and Genuine Parts all anchor the corporate base. Microsoft, Google, Visa, and Mailchimp (now Intuit) have poured billions into Atlanta tech expansion since 2020. "Transaction Alley" - the cluster of payments and fintech firms centered on Alpharetta and Sandy Springs including Global Payments, FIS, NCR Voyix, and a long tail of startups - processes a meaningful share of US electronic payments. Georgia's film tax credit has made Atlanta a production hub that locals call Y'allywood, second only to Los Angeles by shoot days.

The side effect of all that growth is an Atlanta tech talent war that now rivals second-tier Bay Area compensation. Per Built In Atlanta and the Metro Atlanta Chamber 2024-2025 data, senior developers routinely command $160,000+ base, and even mid-level finance and ops roles at top employers push into six figures. For Atlanta SMBs, Transaction Alley startups, and independent production companies, that inflation makes every hire an expensive decision.

Atlanta remote staffing through Teckas gives local operators a way to build out the operations, CX, finance, and junior engineering layers at one-third to one-fourth of loaded local cost, while keeping senior talent on-the-ground in ATL.

How Atlanta's Time Zone Works With India

Atlanta runs on Eastern Time. India Standard Time is 9 hours 30 minutes ahead of ET. An Atlanta 9am to 6pm ET workday maps to 6:30pm to 3:30am IST. Our Atlanta-dedicated staff work that window as their full-time shift, giving your Midtown, Buckhead, or Alpharetta team live coverage during business hours.
