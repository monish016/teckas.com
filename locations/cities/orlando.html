Why Orlando Businesses Hire Remote

Orlando's economy runs on two engines. The first is tourism - Walt Disney World, Universal Orlando, SeaWorld, and the International Drive hospitality cluster drove roughly 74 million visitors to Orlando in the most recent peak year per Visit Orlando data. The second is aerospace, anchored by the Space Coast at Kennedy Space Center, SpaceX's Florida operations, Blue Origin's Exploration Park, and Lockheed Martin's Orlando campus. Between them, Orlando has one of the most seasonally volatile labor markets of any US metro, stacked on top of a rapidly growing aerospace supplier base.

For tourism operators, that volatility is expensive. Hiring a full-time W-2 reservations agent in March to handle summer peak means carrying the cost through September shoulder and October's pre-holiday lull. Laying them off in November is operationally painful and damages retention. For Space Coast suppliers, the challenge is different - they need back-office bandwidth to keep pace with launch cadence without hiring engineers to do admin work.

Orlando remote staffing through Teckas gives both sides flexibility. Our engagements are month-to-month, staff are full-time Teckas employees (not your W-2), and you can flex headcount up or down without labor-law complications.

How Orlando's Time Zone Works With India

Orlando runs on Eastern Time. India Standard Time is 9 hours 30 minutes ahead of ET. An Orlando 8am to 5pm ET workday lands at 5:30pm to 2:30am IST in India. Our team works that shift as their full-time hours, so your reservations agents are live when your hotel front desk needs them, not at 4am Orlando time.
