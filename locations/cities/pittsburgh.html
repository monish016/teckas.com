Why Pittsburgh Businesses Hire Remote

Pittsburgh has quietly reinvented itself as one of the most interesting tech economies in the country. Carnegie Mellon's robotics, machine learning, and computer science programs are widely considered best-in-class, and CMU alumni and spinouts have attracted Google (one of its largest non-California offices is in East Liberty), Uber's Advanced Technologies Group (the seed of its self-driving work), Aurora, Motional, Argo AI, and Duolingo to Pittsburgh. Combined with UPMC - the largest private employer in Pennsylvania at over 92,000 employees - PNC Financial Services, and a dense cluster of advanced manufacturing and steel-adjacent firms, Pittsburgh's labor market looks very different from what it did 20 years ago.

That reinvention has consequences. Pittsburgh engineer and data-science salaries have climbed roughly 20-30 percent over pre-2020 baselines per Built In Pittsburgh and Glassdoor data, and non-tech employers increasingly find themselves bidding against CMU-spinoff startups for the same analyst, data-ops, and admin talent. A Pittsburgh law firm, RIA, medical practice, or small manufacturer trying to hire an operations associate is now competing indirectly with an AI startup that just closed a Series A.

Pittsburgh remote staffing through Teckas gives local operators a way to build out the operations, finance, CX, and admin layer at a fraction of that cost, while keeping engineering, medical, and shop-floor talent in-market.

How Pittsburgh's Time Zone Works With India

Pittsburgh runs on Eastern Time. India Standard Time is 9 hours 30 minutes ahead of ET. A Pittsburgh 8am to 5pm ET workday maps to 5:30pm to 2:30am IST in India. Our staff work that shift as their full-time hours, giving you live Slack and phone coverage during business hours.
