Why Philadelphia Businesses Hire Remote

Philadelphia is an unusual labor market. On one side, it is a healthcare and education heavyweight - Jefferson Health, Penn Medicine, Temple Health, Children's Hospital of Philadelphia (CHOP), Drexel, and the University of Pennsylvania anchor tens of thousands of jobs in Center City, University City, and the Main Line. On the other, it is a mid-market business economy with strong clusters in legal services, financial services (Vanguard in nearby Malvern, fintech across the region), and publishing.

The defining fiscal feature for Philadelphia employers is the Philadelphia Wage Tax. Per the Philadelphia Department of Revenue 2024-2025 rates, residents pay approximately 3.75 percent and nonresidents working inside city limits pay approximately 3.44 percent on top of federal and Pennsylvania state income tax. That levy sits uniquely on Philly wages and makes the loaded cost of a Center City W-2 hire materially higher than an identical hire in King of Prussia or Cherry Hill. Combined with Pennsylvania's standard unemployment and workers' comp structure, every Philly-based salaried role carries a meaningful tax overhead.
