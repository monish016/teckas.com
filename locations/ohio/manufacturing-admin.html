Why Ohio Manufacturers Hire Remote Admins

Ohio is one of the largest manufacturing states in the country. Per the Ohio Manufacturers' Association and BLS data, manufacturing accounts for roughly 17 percent of Ohio GDP, and the state has over 600,000 manufacturing jobs. Cleveland anchors metals, plastics, and medical devices; Cincinnati has Procter & Gamble, Milacron, and a deep machine-tool cluster; Columbus has Honda's Marysville complex and significant automotive supplier activity; Akron still carries the polymer and rubber legacy. Most of these operators are mid-market private companies running Epicor, SAP Business One, NetSuite, Infor CSI, Global Shop, or IQMS.

Ohio manufacturing admin pay per Indeed runs $45,000 to $58,000 per year, or $4,000 to $5,000 loaded. The tight Midwest manufacturing labor market (covered often by Crain's Cleveland and IndustryWeek) means hiring experienced ERP admins takes 2 to 3 months and retention is a fight. A Teckas remote admin works your Eastern Time shift in your ERP, handles vendor AP workflow, PO entry, 3-way match, and production reporting.

BLS May 2023 median for production, planning, and expediting clerks is $54,640.
