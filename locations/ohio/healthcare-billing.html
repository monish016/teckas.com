Why Ohio Practices Hire Remote Healthcare Billers

Ohio is home to some of the largest healthcare systems in the US. Cleveland Clinic, University Hospitals, MetroHealth, OhioHealth, Mercy Health, TriHealth, Nationwide Children's in Columbus, and UC Health in Cincinnati all anchor major medical districts. Around them sit thousands of independent and specialty practices that either refer into these systems or absorb overflow. The referring and specialty practice ecosystem runs on tight billing cycles and can't absorb claim lags.

Ohio Medicaid is administered through MCOs (Molina, CareSource, Buckeye Health Plan, UnitedHealthcare Community Plan, Aetna Better Health, AmeriHealth Caritas) with their own submission portals and prior-auth quirks. Ohio also has a higher-than-average Medicare population, particularly in northeast Ohio counties. Local biller pay per Indeed runs $42,000 to $55,000 per year, or $3,800 to $4,800 loaded.

A Teckas biller works under a signed BAA on your PM/EHR. BLS May 2023 national median for medical records specialists is $47,180.
