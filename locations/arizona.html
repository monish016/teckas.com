Why Arizona Businesses Are Going Remote

Arizona is one of the fastest-growing states in the US by population, with the Phoenix metro now the 10th-largest in the country. US Census data shows Maricopa County adding roughly 80,000 to 100,000 net residents per year. The Arizona Commerce Authority and local chambers have flagged structural shortages in healthcare admin, real estate support, construction back-office, and customer operations. Phoenix hospital systems like Banner Health and HonorHealth are well-documented on their struggle to staff schedulers, billers, and prior-auth specialists. Real estate teams cannot grow transaction coordinator benches fast enough to keep up with the pipeline.

On top of growth, Arizona is now one of the top three US states for utility-scale solar and storage. That has created a wave of solar installer operators, each needing permit coordinators, scheduling ops, and back-office support. Teckas clients in Phoenix and Scottsdale use remote staff to run those functions on Arizona hours at a fraction of the local loaded cost.

How Working in Arizona Mountain Time Works

Arizona is unusual: it sits in the Mountain Time zone but does not observe daylight saving (MST year-round). That means from roughly March to November, Arizona aligns with Pacific Time (PDT), and from November to March, it aligns with Mountain Standard Time. Our India-based staff work your actual business hours either way. We track the DST shift and move the shift window automatically so nothing breaks in March or November. For a 9am to 6pm Arizona shift, staff in India work roughly 8:30pm to 5:30am (summer) or 9:30pm to 6:30am (winter) IST.
