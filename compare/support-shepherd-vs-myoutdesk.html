Support Shepherd vs MyOutDesk: Side-by-Side Comparison

A neutral 2026 comparison of two US-based remote staffing companies sourcing Philippines talent, with very different industry focus and target audiences.

TL;DR Summary

Support Shepherd and MyOutDesk are both US-based managed staffing companies that source primarily from the Philippines. The difference is focus: Support Shepherd covers general roles such as admin, customer service, and operations across many industries. MyOutDesk has a long-standing specialization in real estate, with training, systems, and talent oriented around brokerage and agent workflows. If you are in real estate, MyOutDesk is the more natural default; if you are outside real estate, Support Shepherd is broader.

Support Shepherd Overview

Support Shepherd is a US-based managed staffing company that places remote professionals from the Philippines and Latin America with US businesses across a range of industries. Their model is a flat monthly fee per placed professional, with the placement working full-time and dedicated to a single client. Support Shepherd handles recruiting, initial vetting, and ongoing client success, while the client directs day-to-day work.

The company positions itself as a cost-effective path to full-time remote hires for businesses that would otherwise be stuck between expensive US contractors and harder-to-manage freelance marketplaces. Its catalog leans toward admin assistants, customer service reps, operations coordinators, bookkeeping support, and general virtual assistant roles.

Strengths:

  • Clear flat monthly pricing with no hourly complexity
  • Broad industry coverage across SMB, ecommerce, services
  • Dedicated full-time placements from day one
  • US-based client success reduces time zone friction during setup

Weaknesses:

  • Less specialized depth in vertical playbooks
  • Not the lowest cost option for part-time or highly variable work
  • Primarily Philippines talent limits options for buyers wanting India, Eastern Europe, or other regions

MyOutDesk Overview

MyOutDesk is one of the longest-running US-based providers of Philippines-based virtual professionals, and is especially well known in real estate. Over many years it has built training, role frameworks, and recruiting pipelines oriented toward real estate Inside Sales Agents, transaction coordinators, marketing assistants, and administrative support for brokerages and teams. MyOutDesk also serves non-real-estate clients, but its market recognition is strongest in that vertical.

The company sells subscription placements with ongoing account support and emphasizes compliance, screening, and onboarding for regulated or licensed-adjacent workflows. For a real estate team trying to scale lead response, transaction management, and listing operations, the MyOutDesk catalog reads almost like an in-industry playbook.

Strengths:

  • Deep real estate specialization (ISA, TC, marketing VA, admin)
  • Long track record and brand recognition in real estate
  • Training and screening tuned to vertical workflows
  • Dedicated full-time placements with managed onboarding

Weaknesses:

  • Higher starting price than some general-purpose providers
  • Specialization leans real estate, which may be less relevant to other industries
  • Philippines-only talent region

Detailed Comparison by Category

Pricing Model

Both companies publish subscription pricing rather than hourly. Support Shepherd tends to be positioned as a lower-cost flat monthly fee. MyOutDesk pricing reflects its investment in vertical training and tends to sit at the higher end, especially for specialized real estate roles. Neither company generally bills you separately for time tracking, benefits, or compliance; the monthly price is designed to be all-in for a dedicated placement.

Talent Quality and Region

Both source primarily from the Philippines, which is well suited to English-heavy customer-facing and back-office work. Support Shepherd has also engaged Latin America talent for roles that benefit from Spanish or closer Eastern Time coverage. Quality levels are broadly comparable; the decisive factor is usually vertical fit rather than raw talent quality.

Role Coverage

Support Shepherd covers generalist roles: executive assistant, customer support agent, operations coordinator, data entry, bookkeeping assistant, and similar. MyOutDesk covers those generalist roles too but adds a strong layer of real-estate-specific roles such as inside sales agents trained on real estate lead scripts, transaction coordinators familiar with escrow timelines, and marketing assistants experienced with MLS and listing marketing. For specialized real estate work, MyOutDesk's catalog is harder to match.

Management Approach

Both offer an account management layer with a US-based client success contact. MyOutDesk tends to invest more in vertical training materials and onboarding collateral aligned to real estate operations. Support Shepherd's management layer is lighter-touch and more flexible across industries. Neither fully replaces an internal team lead on the client side, but both provide a recruiter-plus-support wrapper that is noticeably better than a pure marketplace.

Commitment and Flexibility

Both operate on monthly subscriptions with no long-term lock-in. In practice, MyOutDesk clients often plan for longer engagements because the placements become integrated with brokerage systems and client workflows; switching cost grows with depth. Support Shepherd engagements are similarly sticky but less vertical-specific, which can make swaps slightly easier.

Who Support Shepherd Is Best For

Support Shepherd is a strong fit for:

  • SMBs and ecommerce brands needing general admin or customer support
  • Buyers who want a predictable flat monthly fee
  • Teams that prefer a simple, industry-agnostic approach
  • Operations and finance roles that do not need vertical training
  • First-time remote staffing buyers who want a straightforward entry

Who MyOutDesk Is Best For

MyOutDesk is a strong fit for:

  • Real estate brokerages, teams, and top-producing agents
  • Companies needing inside sales agents trained on real estate scripts
  • Teams hiring transaction coordinators familiar with escrow and MLS workflows
  • Buyers who value a long track record and vertical brand recognition
  • Brokerages with compliance-sensitive process needs

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Support Shepherd or MyOutDesk better for real estate?
MyOutDesk has a well-known focus on real estate and has served a large number of brokerages, teams, and individual agents over many years. Their playbooks, training, and candidate pool are oriented toward that industry. Support Shepherd serves a broader range of industries and is less specialized on real estate workflows, though it can fill general admin or customer support roles that a real estate team might also need.
Do both companies source from the Philippines?
Yes. Both Support Shepherd and MyOutDesk are US-based companies that primarily source remote talent from the Philippines. Support Shepherd has also worked with Latin America talent in some roles. Both operate a recruit-and-place model with ongoing client support.
Which has simpler pricing?
Both publish flat monthly rates rather than hourly. Exact figures vary by seniority and role. Support Shepherd's model is often positioned as a straightforward flat monthly fee per placed professional. MyOutDesk uses subscription pricing that may vary by tier and role, including real estate specific role tiers. Buyers should request current quotes from each to compare like for like.
What if I want India-based talent instead?
Both Support Shepherd and MyOutDesk focus on Philippines talent. Companies that specifically want India-based professionals (often for engineering, finance, analytics, or senior back-office roles) typically look at India-focused managed staffing providers such as Teckas. The model is similar in spirit but different in talent region and role depth.
