BELAY vs Time Etc: VA Service Comparison

A neutral comparison of two premium virtual assistant services for US and UK businesses, covering talent region, pricing model, and commitment structure.

TL;DR Summary

BELAY and Time Etc both sit in the premium, onshore virtual assistant category, but their models differ. BELAY places US-based contractors on monthly plans built around consistent, deep executive support. Time Etc offers UK and US-based assistants on hourly block pricing, with roughly 20 years of operating history and a task-flexibility model that suits buyers who want a premium VA without a rigid monthly commitment.

BELAY Overview

BELAY is a US-based remote staffing company that places US contractors as executive assistants, bookkeepers, social media managers, and website specialists. It targets founders, executives, ministries, nonprofits, and growing businesses that want a consistent onshore assistant rather than an offshore one. BELAY handles matching, onboarding, and ongoing client success, and it is known for a careful matching process that reduces early mismatches.

Because BELAY's talent pool is US-based and its placements work at contractor rates set by US market conditions, it sits firmly in the premium tier. The pitch is simple: you pay more than you would for an offshore provider, and in exchange you get onshore time zones, native English, cultural proximity, and a highly curated match.

Strengths:

  • US-based contractors; full time-zone and cultural alignment
  • Careful matching process across a range of specialized roles
  • Strong brand recognition among US executives and nonprofits
  • Managed account support for ongoing fit and escalations

Weaknesses:

  • Premium pricing; not a cost-savings play
  • Hours are capped by plan; heavy workloads can outgrow the model
  • Less attractive for buyers focused primarily on cost reduction

Time Etc Overview

Time Etc is one of the longest-running virtual assistant companies in the market, established roughly 20 years ago in the UK and serving clients in both the UK and US. Its VAs are vetted through a multi-step screening process and assigned as a dedicated match to each client. Time Etc's differentiator is its hourly block pricing: clients buy a monthly hour bundle and can flex how those hours are spent across different tasks and, in some cases, different VAs when specialization is needed.

Time Etc suits executives and small businesses that want high-quality onshore support without committing to a full-time headcount. Its long operating history provides a level of reliability signal that newer providers cannot match.

Strengths:

  • Roughly 20 years of operating history
  • Hourly block model gives flexibility month to month
  • UK and US talent aligned to Western business culture
  • Strong screening and dedicated VA assignment

Weaknesses:

  • Still premium priced relative to offshore options
  • Hourly model requires thoughtful task queuing
  • Smaller specialist role catalog compared with broader staffing firms

Detailed Comparison by Category

Pricing Model

BELAY uses monthly plan pricing built around a set number of hours per month of dedicated support. It is designed for buyers who want predictable cost and a consistent weekly cadence. Time Etc uses hourly block pricing, with clients buying a monthly hour bundle and flexing across tasks and weeks. For someone whose workload varies week to week, Time Etc's model generally fits better; for someone who wants a consistent weekly rhythm of dedicated support, BELAY's plan model is cleaner.

Talent Quality and Region

Both companies compete on talent quality rather than price. BELAY's US-based contractor model gives full US time-zone coverage and native English. Time Etc's UK and US model covers both markets, with UK VAs typically serving UK daytime and US VAs covering US hours. For a US executive who wants morning-to-evening coverage without time-zone gymnastics, BELAY is a natural default. For a UK-based founder or for cross-Atlantic teams, Time Etc is a strong fit.

Role Coverage

BELAY covers a targeted set of premium roles: executive assistants, bookkeepers, social media managers, and web specialists. Time Etc focuses more squarely on executive and general administrative support, with research, scheduling, inbox, and content administration among the most common task types. If you need bookkeeping or social media execution specifically, BELAY's specialization is deeper; if you need general executive support with flexibility, Time Etc's model is well-suited.

Management Approach

Both companies provide a relationship manager layer and handle replacement if the initial match does not work. BELAY is known for investing heavily in the matching process upfront, which tends to reduce mid-engagement swaps. Time Etc's approach is lighter-touch and leans on its long history of VA assignment, with flexibility to re-match as needs evolve.

Commitment and Flexibility

BELAY is built around ongoing monthly plans that work best when the relationship runs for many months. Time Etc's hourly block model gives more flexibility to dial hours up or down month over month. For one-off projects or variable workloads, Time Etc is more accommodating; for a long-term "always there" assistant, BELAY is more natural.

Who BELAY Is Best For

BELAY is a strong fit for:

  • US executives, founders, and business owners wanting onshore support
  • Nonprofits and ministries that prioritize cultural alignment
  • Buyers needing bookkeeping, social media, or web VA specialization
  • Teams willing to pay premium onshore rates for consistency
  • Leaders who want a carefully matched long-term assistant relationship

Who Time Etc Is Best For

Time Etc is a strong fit for:

  • UK-based founders and executives
  • US buyers who like hourly block flexibility over fixed monthly plans
  • Small businesses with variable weekly workloads
  • Buyers who value a long operating history and brand trust
  • Clients whose needs are primarily executive and general admin support

Frequently Asked Questions

Does BELAY use US-based contractors?
Yes. BELAY is known for placing US-based contractors as virtual assistants, bookkeepers, social media managers, and executive assistants. That is one of its primary differentiators and a reason it sits at the premium end of the VA market on price.
Where are Time Etc VAs located?
Time Etc operates in both the UK and the US and places virtual assistants from those markets. The company has been running for roughly 20 years and uses an hourly block pricing model where clients purchase monthly hour bundles they can use flexibly.
Which is more affordable?
Both are positioned at the premium end compared with offshore providers, because both use US or UK talent rather than offshoring. On a per-hour basis, Time Etc's hourly block bundles can be lower commitment than BELAY's typical monthly plans, but BELAY's model is designed for deeper, more consistent support. Neither aims to compete on raw hourly rate with Philippines or India providers.
What if I want offshore-priced talent with managed oversight?
If a primary driver is lower cost while still having a managed provider handle HR, payroll, and replacement, India-based or Philippines-based managed staffing providers are worth considering. Teckas, for example, prices dedicated India-based full-time professionals at flat monthly rates well below typical BELAY or Time Etc pricing, while covering employment and HR. The tradeoff is talent region and culture fit.
