BruntWork vs Wing Assistant: Full 2026 Comparison

A neutral side-by-side look at pricing, talent region, dedicated vs pool staffing, and management model between two well-known remote staffing providers.

TL;DR Summary

BruntWork and Wing Assistant both deliver remote staffing with Philippines as their primary talent market, but their commercial models diverge sharply. BruntWork prices by the hour and leans toward flexible, variable engagements. Wing Assistant prices by tiered monthly subscription with defined task scopes, leaning toward structured, long-term assistant relationships. Pick BruntWork for hourly flexibility; pick Wing for predictable monthly costs and a managed product experience.

BruntWork Overview

BruntWork is an Australia-headquartered outsourcing provider with a large Philippines-based agent workforce. It is best known for its broad role catalog - customer support, data entry, admin, telesales, bookkeeping, healthcare back office - and its hourly pricing transparency. Customers typically engage BruntWork for one or more dedicated agents who work a defined weekly schedule, billed hourly.

The appeal of BruntWork is simplicity: browse the role catalog, pick the role and hours you need, and start. There is no complex tiering and no long-term lock-in for most engagements, which is attractive to small businesses and support-heavy teams that scale volume up and down.

Strengths:

  • Low entry price per hour (publicly listed at approximately $4 to $10/hr)
  • Large role catalog covering general support and back-office work
  • Hourly model suits variable or seasonal workloads
  • Straightforward procurement without custom contracts

Weaknesses:

  • Client carries more day-to-day management responsibility
  • Quality can vary; careful interviewing matters
  • Hourly billing requires time tracking oversight
  • Less structured onboarding compared to subscription peers

Wing Assistant Overview

Wing Assistant is a US-operated managed virtual assistant company that places assistants sourced primarily from the Philippines, with a growing Latin America option. It sells a tiered subscription product: lower tiers for part-time help, mid tiers for dedicated monthly assistants, higher tiers for executive-level or specialized work. Wing promotes its internal platform, task queue, and account manager layer as a key differentiator.

Wing's pitch is "hire an assistant, skip the hiring headache." The product is positioned to feel like software: sign up, define tasks, and your assistant is introduced quickly with ongoing supervision from Wing's internal team.

Strengths:

  • Predictable flat monthly pricing
  • Managed layer with internal QA and account management
  • LatAm option for nearshore time zones or Spanish speakers
  • Fast onboarding and replacement flow

Weaknesses:

  • Higher monthly starting price than pure hourly options
  • Lower tiers may be shared or part-time rather than dedicated full-time
  • Task scopes can feel templated at entry tiers
  • Less flexibility to scale hours up and down week by week

Detailed Comparison by Category

Pricing Model

BruntWork's hourly model means you pay for exactly the hours used, which is appealing for teams with uneven weekly demand. The downside is that every hour counts; overtime, training time, and downtime are all billed. Wing Assistant's subscription rolls all of that into one monthly price, which is easier to budget but can be more expensive if your actual utilization is low. On a 40-hour full-time basis, the two models often land in a similar zone, with BruntWork skewing slightly cheaper on raw rate and Wing often pricing in more wraparound services.

Talent Quality and Region

Both companies draw from the same broad Philippines talent market, which is deep in English-language customer support, admin, and back-office skills. Wing's Latin America option opens up later US time-zone coverage and Spanish-language support. BruntWork's Philippines focus is proven for overnight US coverage. Quality at both depends heavily on how carefully the client interviews and scopes the role; neither company should be treated as a substitute for your own due diligence during the candidate interview.

Role Coverage

BruntWork lists a wide catalog of role types across customer service, general virtual assistance, bookkeeping, healthcare back office, real estate support, and more. Wing Assistant concentrates on executive assistance, general admin, sales development support, and marketing operations. If your need is a specialist like a bookkeeper or a tele-caller, BruntWork's catalog tends to match more directly. If your need is a general-purpose assistant or an SDR, Wing's product is more purpose-built.

Management Approach

Wing Assistant invests in a visible managed layer: account managers, quality oversight, and internal playbooks. This works well for buyers who want to outsource not only the work but also the supervision. BruntWork's model is lighter; you get the agent and a basic client success contact, but day-to-day coaching and SOP creation are more often your responsibility. Neither model fully replaces an internal team lead, but Wing comes closer for clients that prefer less hands-on oversight.

Commitment and Flexibility

BruntWork is generally easier to scale hours up and down week by week without renegotiating a contract. Wing's subscription is month-to-month in most tiers but is less flexible on hour-level changes mid-month. For a launch period where volume is unpredictable, BruntWork's hourly billing protects against paying for unused capacity. For a steady-state full-time assistant relationship, Wing's fixed monthly cost simplifies accounting.

Who BruntWork Is Best For

BruntWork is a strong fit for:

  • Support teams with variable daily or weekly volume
  • Ecommerce brands running seasonal campaigns
  • Small businesses that want one or two agents quickly at a low hourly rate
  • Buyers comfortable directing work and managing time tracking themselves
  • Teams that need Philippines overnight coverage on a budget

Who Wing Assistant Is Best For

Wing Assistant fits well for:

  • Founders and executives who want a dedicated assistant without hiring one internally
  • Growing teams that prefer predictable monthly budgets
  • Buyers who want a managed layer of oversight, not just a placed agent
  • Companies that value a software-style product experience around their staffing vendor
  • Use cases that benefit from a LatAm option for US time-zone or Spanish coverage

Frequently Asked Questions

Is BruntWork cheaper than Wing Assistant?
It depends on the role and how many hours you need. BruntWork's publicly listed hourly rates start at approximately $4 to $10 per hour, which can come out lower if you only need part-time or variable hours. Wing Assistant uses a tiered monthly subscription publicly listed at approximately $599 to $2,400 per month, which tends to be more predictable for full-time dedicated work. For 40 hours a week, the two often land in a similar range once you add management overhead to BruntWork hours.
Do BruntWork and Wing Assistant both use Philippines talent?
Both companies source primarily from the Philippines. Wing Assistant also publicly promotes a Latin America talent pool for clients who prefer nearshore time zones or Spanish-language support. BruntWork's public positioning is more Philippines-centric.
Which one is better for dedicated full-time hires?
Wing Assistant's structured subscription tiers are designed around dedicated assistants with defined task scopes, which many buyers prefer for full-time work. BruntWork also supports dedicated full-time assignments, but its hourly model is more frequently chosen by companies with variable or campaign-based workloads.
What if neither model fits my needs?
If you want a dedicated full-time professional with managed HR, legal employment, and a flat monthly rate, but prefer India-based talent over Philippines or LatAm, a managed staffing provider such as Teckas is worth evaluating. The model is different from both BruntWork's hourly approach and Wing's tiered subscription.
