What a Remote Paralegal for a US Law Firm Does

A remote paralegal for US law firm work is a trained legal professional who supports your attorneys with research, drafting, document review, filing, and administrative case work, entirely under licensed attorney supervision. Remote paralegals do not practice law. They do not give legal advice, set fees, or represent clients. They extend your attorneys' capacity so billable time is spent where it belongs, on client strategy and advocacy.

According to the US Bureau of Labor Statistics, paralegals and legal assistants earn a median of roughly $60,970 per year, with experienced litigation paralegals in major metros often earning well above that. Outsource legal research and case admin to a managed remote paralegal, and a solo or mid-size firm can scale leverage without adding six-figure overhead.

Key Responsibilities of a Remote Paralegal

  • Legal research across Westlaw, LexisNexis, Fastcase, and Bloomberg Law
  • Drafting research memos, first-draft pleadings, motions, and discovery responses for attorney review
  • Document review, indexing, and privilege log preparation
  • Bates stamping, redaction, and trial binder preparation
  • Cite checking and Bluebook citation formatting
  • Filing preparation for PACER, CM/ECF, and state e-filing portals (attorney signs and files)
  • Client intake, conflict checks, and engagement letter preparation
  • Case management updates in Clio, MyCase, Smokeball, or PracticePanther
  • Medical record requests and summarization for personal injury files
  • Deposition preparation, summaries, and exhibit organization
  • Calendar and deadline tracking, court docket monitoring
  • Billing support, timekeeping entry, and trust accounting data entry

Frequently Asked Questions

Are remote paralegals allowed to practice law?
No. Paralegals cannot practice law, give legal advice, set fees, or represent clients in court. That is true whether the paralegal is in-house or remote. Teckas paralegals work strictly under the supervision of a licensed US attorney at your firm, who reviews and is responsible for all work product delivered to clients or filed with a court.
How do you protect client confidentiality?
Every paralegal signs an NDA and a confidentiality agreement before onboarding. We issue encrypted laptops, require VPN access to your firm systems, apply least-privilege permissions in Clio, MyCase, or NetDocuments, and maintain a clean-desk and no-personal-device policy for client matters. Your firm retains full ownership of all work product.
What legal tech do your paralegals know?
Our virtual paralegal services team has experience in Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther, Smokeball, Filevine, CosmoLex, NetDocuments, iManage, and Worldox. For research they use Westlaw, LexisNexis, Fastcase, Bloomberg Law, and Google Scholar. For filing they work with PACER, CM/ECF, and state e-filing portals.
What kinds of cases can a remote paralegal support?

If your firm has a specialty, let us know on the discovery call and we will match you with a paralegal who has relevant prior experience.
