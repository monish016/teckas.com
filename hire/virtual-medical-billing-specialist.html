What a Virtual Medical Billing Specialist Does

A virtual medical billing specialist is a remote team member who works inside your practice management system to submit claims, verify insurance eligibility, manage denials, post payments, and keep your AR aging under control. For solo practices, group practices, and multi-location clinics across the US, a dedicated remote medical billing specialist is often the difference between a 30-day and a 90-day days-in-AR number, and between clean and chronically underpaid claims.

According to BLS.gov, medical records and health information specialists in the US earn a median of roughly $48,780 per year, and experienced billers often exceed that. Medical billing outsourcing USA through Teckas lets you staff the same function with a full-time, trained specialist at a fraction of local cost, without sacrificing accountability.

Key Responsibilities of a Remote Medical Billing Specialist

  • Charge entry and claim creation from provider documentation
  • Claim scrubbing for CPT, ICD-10-CM, HCPCS, and modifier accuracy
  • Electronic claim submission via Availity, Waystar, Change Healthcare, or Office Ally
  • Insurance eligibility and benefits verification before visits
  • Prior authorization tracking and follow-up
  • Payment posting from ERAs, EOBs, and patient payments
  • Denial management, root-cause analysis, and appeals
  • AR follow-up on unpaid claims aged 30, 60, and 90 days
  • Patient statement generation and self-pay collections support
  • Credentialing and payer enrollment support
  • Month-end revenue cycle reports for the practice manager

Tools and Systems Your Remote Medical Billing Specialist Knows

  • EHR and practice management - Epic, Athenahealth, Kareo (Tebra), eClinicalWorks, AdvancedMD, NextGen, DrChrono, Practice Fusion
  • Clearinghouses - Availity, Waystar, Change Healthcare, Office Ally, Trizetto
  • Coding references - AAPC and AMA code sets, AHA Coding Clinic, payer-specific policies
  • Productivity - Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Slack, Microsoft Teams
  • Patient communication - secure messaging portals, Klara, Spruce, Weave

Frequently Asked Questions

Are your medical billing specialists HIPAA certified?
HIPAA is a US law that applies to covered entities and their business associates, not a certification individuals hold. Our specialists are trained in HIPAA-aware workflows, sign Business Associate Agreements where required, use company-issued devices with full-disk encryption, and follow least-privilege access to PHI. Your practice remains the covered entity and controls all access.
Which practice management and EHR systems do you support?
Our remote medical billing specialists have hands-on experience in Epic, Athenahealth, Kareo (Tebra), eClinicalWorks, AdvancedMD, NextGen, DrChrono, and Practice Fusion. For clearinghouses we work with Availity, Waystar, Change Healthcare, and Office Ally.
Can your billers perform medical coding?
Our specialists have working familiarity with CPT, ICD-10-CM, and HCPCS codes for claim review, scrubbing, and denial management. For complex coding that requires CPC, COC, or CCS credentials, we can recruit a certified coder on request. Let us know your coding needs on the discovery call.
