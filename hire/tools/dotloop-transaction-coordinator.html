What a Dotloop Transaction Coordinator Does

A Dotloop transaction coordinator is the person who stands between a ratified contract and a clean closing file. They open the loop, pull in the right template documents, track e-signatures, coordinate with title, lender, co-op agent, and inspector, keep the task template moving, flag compliance gaps, and hand your managing broker a loop that audits cleanly. For a high-volume agent or team running 50 to 300 sides a year, a good TC is the difference between a scalable operation and a founder trapped in paperwork.

The TCs Teckas places already know Dotloop. They know the shape of the loop, the difference between a document template and a task template, how to fix a stuck signature request, and how to structure folders so your broker can audit in under 10 minutes.

Core Workflows Your Dotloop TC Owns

  • Opening new loops from contract ratification using your template library
  • Preparing and routing documents for e-signature to buyer, seller, co-op agent, and title
  • Running the task template from contract to close: EMD, inspection, appraisal, loan, clear to close
  • Compliance review against your brokerage checklist
  • Coordinating with title, lender, co-op, and inspector via loop messaging or email
  • Tracking contingency dates, earnest money deposit, and closing disclosures
  • Chasing stuck signatures and missing documents
  • Preparing the loop for broker review and submitting to compliance
  • Commission disbursement authorization preparation
  • Post-close: archiving, client gift coordination, and CRM stage update
  • Weekly pipeline reports for the team lead or managing broker
