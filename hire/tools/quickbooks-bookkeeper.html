What a QuickBooks Bookkeeper for Hire Does

A QuickBooks bookkeeper for hire is a dedicated remote team member who lives inside your QuickBooks Online or QuickBooks Desktop file every day, categorizing transactions, reconciling bank and credit card accounts, chasing AR, paying bills, and closing the books every month. They are not a part-time freelancer who logs in twice a month. They are a full-time employee assigned to your business, with a backup team behind them so your books never go dark.

For small and mid-sized businesses running QuickBooks, bookkeeping is the single most outsourced back-office function in the US and Canada, and for good reason. The US Bureau of Labor Statistics puts the median annual wage for bookkeeping, accounting, and auditing clerks at $47,440, and a local hire also means payroll tax, benefits, PTO, and recruiting costs on top. A managed remote QuickBooks bookkeeper gives you the same output for roughly a third of the fully loaded cost.

Core Workflows Your QuickBooks Bookkeeper Runs

  • Daily categorization of bank feed transactions in QBO using bank rules you approve
  • Accounts receivable: invoicing, statement runs, past-due follow-ups, and cash application
  • Accounts payable: bill entry through Bill.com or Hubdoc, approval routing, and scheduled payments
  • Monthly bank and credit card reconciliation for all connected accounts
  • Payroll journal entries synced from Gusto, ADP, QuickBooks Payroll, or Wagepoint for Canadian clients
  • Sales tax tracking and filing preparation for US state sales tax and Canadian GST, HST, and PST
  • Class, location, and project tracking for multi-department or multi-property books
  • Inventory adjustments, COGS reconciliation, and landed cost entries
  • 1099 vendor tracking through the year and year-end 1099 preparation
  • Month-end close: accrual adjustments, prepaids, deferred revenue, depreciation, and close checklist
  • Monthly financial reporting packs: P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, AR aging, and AP aging

QuickBooks-Specific Skills They Bring

We do not place generic bookkeepers on QuickBooks engagements. Every bookkeeper assigned to your account is screened for hands-on QBO experience and many hold the QuickBooks Online ProAdvisor certification. Expect fluency in:

  • QuickBooks Online - Simple Start, Essentials, Plus, and Advanced tiers, including multi-currency and class tracking
  • QuickBooks Desktop - Pro, Premier, Enterprise, and Desktop hosted on Rightworks or Ace Cloud
  • Bank rules and recurring transactions to automate categorization once patterns are established
  • Undeposited funds workflow and merchant deposit matching for Stripe, Square, and PayPal
  • Accountant tools: Accountant's Copy, journal entry batch upload, and reclassify transactions
  • Reports customization with memorized reports, management reports, and custom report groups
  • Budgeting and forecasting inside QBO Advanced for clients who need a planning view

Related Tools They Can Work With

QuickBooks rarely stands alone. Our bookkeepers are comfortable inside the full modern accounting stack, so you do not need to hire a separate specialist for each integration:

  • Bill.com and Melio for AP workflow and approval routing
  • Hubdoc, Dext, and AutoEntry for receipt and bill capture
  • Gusto, ADP Run, Rippling, and QuickBooks Payroll for US payroll posting
  • Wagepoint and Payworks for Canadian payroll integration
  • Expensify, Ramp, Brex, and Divvy for expense management and credit card reconciliation
  • Shopify, Amazon, Stripe, Square, and PayPal feeds for ecommerce revenue reconciliation
  • Fathom, Spotlight, and LivePlan for client-facing reporting and forecasts
  • Karbon, Jetpack Workflow, and Financial Cents for firm workflow if you are an accounting practice
