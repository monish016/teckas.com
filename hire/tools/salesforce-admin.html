What a Remote Salesforce Admin Does

A remote Salesforce admin is a full-time, dedicated Salesforce specialist who owns the day-to-day health of your org. They build and maintain Flows, configure objects and page layouts, write reports and dashboards, clean and dedupe data, manage integrations into the wider sales and marketing stack, and serve as the first point of contact when a sales rep or CS manager needs something changed in Salesforce today, not next sprint.

For SaaS companies between Series A and Series C, a full-time local Salesforce admin in the US is one of the most expensive hires on a RevOps team. Glassdoor and Indeed list certified Salesforce admin salaries at $85,000 to $115,000 per year, which is roughly $7,000 to $9,500 per month before benefits and payroll tax. Most companies that size do not need a US-based admin 40 hours a week, but they do need someone owning the org full time. A Teckas admin is that person at a fraction of the cost.

Core Workflows Your Salesforce Admin Owns

  • User setup, profile and permission set maintenance, and license management
  • Custom object, field, page layout, and record type configuration
  • Flow Builder development for record-triggered, screen, scheduled, and subflows
  • Migration from legacy Process Builder and Workflow Rules
  • Validation rules, approval processes, and assignment rules
  • Report and dashboard development for pipeline, forecast, activity, and CS health
  • Data loading, mass updates, and dedupe with Data Loader, Cloudingo, or Demandtools
  • Integration management for HubSpot, Outreach, Salesloft, Gong, Marketo, DocuSign, Chili Piper
  • Sandbox refreshes, deployment with change sets or Gearset, and release management
  • End-user support tickets through your internal helpdesk
  • Documentation in Confluence, Notion, or your internal wiki
