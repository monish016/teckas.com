What a ServiceTitan Dispatcher Actually Does

A ServiceTitan dispatcher is a remote team member who lives inside the ServiceTitan dispatch board all day, booking inbound calls, assigning jobs to the right technician by skill and geography, rebalancing the board as the day changes, confirming customers, chasing invoices, and pulling the reports your operations manager reviews every morning. They know the shortcuts, the capacity planning tool, the zones setup, and the difference between a job, an opportunity, and a membership visit.

For a home services company running 5 to 40 trucks on ServiceTitan, the dispatcher seat is the revenue hub of the business. Every inbound call is real money, and every misrouted truck is lost billable hours. Hiring a local ServiceTitan-trained dispatcher in the US usually means $4,000 to $5,500 per month plus benefits, and a long learning curve. A managed remote ServiceTitan dispatcher from Teckas shows up already fluent in the system.

ServiceTitan Workflows Your Dispatcher Owns

  • Booking inbound calls using the Call Booking screen with the correct business unit, job type, and campaign tracking
  • Dispatch board management across zones, shifts, and skill tags
  • Capacity planning for tune-up season and maintenance sweeps
  • Tech routing with drive time awareness and priority job handling
  • Confirmation calls, day-before reminders, and on-the-way notifications
  • Reassigning jobs when techs run over or cancellations open the schedule
  • Membership visit scheduling and renewal outreach
  • Invoicing support: closing out jobs, applying price book items, and collecting balances
  • Customer record hygiene: tags, notes, equipment, and service history
  • Daily and weekly reporting: booked calls, converted opportunities, revenue per tech
  • Escalating no-heat, no-cool, water leak, and other emergency priority calls per your SLA
