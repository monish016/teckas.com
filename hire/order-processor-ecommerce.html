What an Ecommerce Order Processor Does for Your Brand

An ecommerce order processor remote hire is the operational hub between your storefront, your 3PL, your ad spend, and your customer. They review every order for fraud and address issues, handle manual orders from wholesale and B2B channels, push orders to the warehouse, resolve fulfillment exceptions, and manage the full returns and exchanges cycle. For a growing ecommerce brand, they are the difference between a clean P&L and a pile of unresolved tickets.

Teckas places dedicated remote order processors with hands-on Shopify, Amazon, and 3PL experience. They work in your platform, on your SOPs, under your brand voice. Most ecommerce clients free up 15 to 25 hours per week for the founder or ops manager within the first month.

Key Responsibilities of a Remote Ecommerce Order Processor

  • Review and release daily Shopify, Amazon, and WooCommerce orders
  • Flag fraud and address verification issues before shipment
  • Enter manual, B2B, and wholesale orders into the storefront or ERP
  • Coordinate with the 3PL on exceptions, backorders, and split shipments
  • Process returns, exchanges, and refunds in Loop, Returnly, or Shopify
  • Open and resolve Amazon Seller Central cases (stranded inventory, FBA issues)
  • Update tracking and proactively notify customers on delays
  • Reconcile daily shipments against platform order counts
  • Support customer service team with order-status tickets in Gorgias or Zendesk
  • Maintain the SKU master, stock updates, and pre-order flags
  • Pull daily order, fulfillment, and returns reports for ops

Frequently Asked Questions

What does an ecommerce order processor do?
An ecommerce order processor handles daily order review, manual order entry, returns and exchanges, fulfillment coordination with your warehouse or 3PL, and customer communication on shipping delays and stock issues. They keep the order pipeline clean from cart to delivery.
Which ecommerce platforms do your order processors know?
Our remote order processors have daily experience with Shopify, Shopify Plus, Amazon Seller Central, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Magento, eBay, Etsy, and Walmart Marketplace. They also know ShipStation, ShipBob, Gorgias, Zendesk, and common 3PL portals.
Can a remote order processor handle returns and refunds?
Yes. Our order processors handle the full returns and exchanges workflow: issuing RMAs in Loop or Returnly, processing refunds in Shopify or Amazon per your policy, coordinating with the 3PL on inbound receiving, and updating the customer. They follow your SOP, not an offshore script.

Full-time remote order processors through Teckas start at $1,200 per month, all-in. Per BLS and Indeed data, a US-based ecommerce order processor or fulfillment coordinator typically costs $40,000 to $52,000 per year plus benefits.
Can they work Amazon Seller Central case management?
Yes. Our processors open and respond to Amazon Seller Central cases including stranded inventory, FBA shipment issues, A-to-z claims, and listing errors. They escalate anything requiring brand-level decisions to your team. Book a call to scope the role.
