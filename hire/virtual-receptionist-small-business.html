Why Small Businesses Hire a Virtual Receptionist

If you run a small business in the US or Canada, missed calls are missed revenue. Industry research consistently shows that most callers who reach voicemail simply call the next business on their list, and that response rate drops sharply after the first few hours. A virtual receptionist for small business solves that by answering every inbound call, booking qualified appointments, and capturing lead details into your CRM, without the cost of a full-time in-office hire.

Teckas places a dedicated remote receptionist USA and Canada clients can rely on: one person, assigned to your business, trained on your greeting, your scripts, and your tools. Not a shared-pool virtual phone answering service, and not a freelancer juggling five clients.

Key Responsibilities of a Virtual Receptionist

  • Inbound call answering with your company greeting and brand voice
  • Appointment scheduling in Calendly, Acuity, Setmore, or native calendars
  • Lead qualification and intake form completion
  • Warm transfers and intelligent call routing to the right team member
  • Voicemail management and rapid callback on missed calls
  • CRM updates in HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho, Pipedrive, Keap, or Go High Level
  • Email inbox triage and first-response handling
  • Live chat and SMS response support
  • Payment reminders and appointment confirmations
  • Customer service follow-ups and review requests
  • Overflow and after-hours coverage during peaks
  • Daily call logs and weekly reporting to the owner

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a virtual receptionist and a phone answering service?
A virtual phone answering service typically uses a shared pool of agents answering calls for many different companies, often with per-minute pricing. A Teckas virtual receptionist is a dedicated person who works only for your business during their shift, learns your scripts, uses your CRM, and knows your team. You get the consistency of an in-house receptionist at a fraction of the cost.
Do you offer 24/7 virtual receptionist coverage?
Yes. We can staff multiple receptionists across shifts for daytime, evening, weekend, and overnight coverage. Many small businesses pair a dedicated daytime receptionist with a part-time evening or overflow backup to achieve 24/7 coverage without hiring a full team locally.
Which tools and CRMs do your virtual receptionists use?
Our remote receptionists work in tools like RingCentral, OpenPhone, Dialpad, Aircall, Grasshopper, Google Voice, and 8x8 for calls, and HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho, Pipedrive, Go High Level, Keap, Monday, and ClickUp for CRM and scheduling. For booking we use Calendly, Acuity, Setmore, and native calendars in Google and Microsoft 365.
How much does a virtual receptionist for a small business cost?
A dedicated full-time virtual receptionist through Teckas starts at $1,200 per month, all-inclusive. The US Bureau of Labor Statistics reports the median annual wage for receptionists at roughly $37,880, or about $3,156 per month, before benefits, payroll taxes, and office space. See full pricing.
Will callers know my receptionist is remote?
Only if you want them to. Your receptionist answers in your company name, follows your greeting script, uses your phone system, and handles callers exactly as an in-office receptionist would.
