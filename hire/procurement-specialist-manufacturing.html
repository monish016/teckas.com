What a Remote Procurement Specialist Does for Your Manufacturing Business

A remote procurement specialist for manufacturing is the person who keeps parts flowing to the floor. They issue purchase orders against MRP demand, acknowledge confirmations, manage expediting, reconcile receiving against POs, chase vendor acknowledgments, resolve invoice discrepancies with AP, and keep item and vendor masters clean in the ERP. For a mid-size manufacturer running 500 to 5,000 active SKUs, this is 30 to 50 hours per week of structured work that a US buyer should not be doing manually.

Teckas places full-time dedicated procurement specialists trained on the major manufacturing ERPs. They work under the direction of your procurement manager, operations manager, or CFO, execute cleanly, and free your US team to focus on strategic sourcing, supplier relationships, and cost reduction.

Key Responsibilities of a Remote Procurement Specialist

  • Convert MRP planned orders to purchase orders in SAP, NetSuite, or Dynamics
  • Send POs to suppliers and chase acknowledgments
  • Maintain expected delivery dates and expedite past-due orders
  • Run daily open PO reports and flag risk-to-production items
  • Match receiving to POs and resolve quantity or SKU mismatches
  • Work AP and receiving on 3-way match invoice exceptions
  • Issue RMAs and vendor returns for defective or over-shipped material
  • Maintain item master: lead time, MOQ, pricing, preferred vendor
  • Maintain vendor master: remit-to, payment terms, contacts, certifications
  • Support RFQ packaging and quote comparison for tactical buys
  • Track spend against budget by category and by cost center

Why Manufacturers Outsource Procurement Support

Manufacturing procurement teams are structurally understaffed. Reshoring, tariff volatility, and supplier consolidation over the past five years has pushed more work onto smaller teams. According to ISM and Gartner surveys, procurement teams report spending 60 to 70 percent of their time on transactional PO and expediting activity rather than strategic sourcing. That is exactly the work a remote procurement specialist handles well.

Outsourcing the tactical buyer layer gives your US procurement manager room to negotiate contracts, qualify second sources, and actually reduce unit cost. It is the difference between procurement as a cost center and procurement as a margin lever.
