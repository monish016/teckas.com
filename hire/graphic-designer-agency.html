What a Remote Graphic Designer Does for Your Agency

A remote graphic designer for agencies is dedicated billable production capacity for brand, social, web, and print work. They pick up creative briefs from your strategist or PM, execute inside Figma and Adobe, route work through your internal review, and ship white-label deliverables to your clients. For a growing agency, a dedicated offshore designer is the difference between turning down retainer expansion and taking it.

Teckas places full-time dedicated graphic designers who work only for your agency. They attend your creative standups, absorb your brand playbooks per client, and match your house style within 30 days.

Key Responsibilities of a Remote Agency Graphic Designer

  • Social graphics and ad creative for Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok, and Pinterest
  • Display and programmatic ad variations in required sizes and formats
  • Landing page and web hero design in Figma with dev-ready handoff
  • Email template design and Klaviyo or Mailchimp block systems
  • Brand identity support: logo variants, color, typography, guidelines
  • Pitch decks and client-facing presentations
  • Print: one-pagers, trade show graphics, direct mail, packaging basics
  • Infographic and report design for content teams
  • Light motion and animated social assets in After Effects or Canva
  • Icon sets, illustration, and stock asset sourcing
  • File organization, versioning, and DAM hygiene

Why Agencies Outsource Graphic Design

Agency creative volume does not match up cleanly with US hiring. One month you are slammed with BFCM campaigns, the next month you are waiting on a brand direction. Hiring a local mid-level designer at $6,500 to $8,500 per month fully loaded only works if you can keep them 90 percent utilized year-round. Most agencies cannot.

A white label graphic designer from Teckas gives you flexible production capacity at a fraction of the cost. Many agencies run a structure of one US art director or design lead plus two to four offshore production designers. That keeps quality gated on the US side while margin stays healthy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a remote graphic designer do for an agency?
A remote graphic designer for agencies produces brand, social, web, and print deliverables for your clients. They work from creative briefs, inside your agency PM tools, and deliver white-label under your brand. Typical output includes social graphics, ad creative, landing page visuals, brand systems, and presentations.
Which design tools do your designers use?
Our remote designers are fluent in Figma, Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, After Effects for light motion, and Canva Pro for templated social. They also use Frame.io and Loom for review cycles and Slack for daily collaboration.
Can designers keep up with an agency-speed revision workflow?
Yes. Our designers are trained on the reality of agency work: tight turns, multiple concurrent clients, and client-driven revisions. We staff designers whose portfolios show volume and speed, not just hero pieces.
How much does a remote agency graphic designer cost?
Full-time dedicated graphic designers through Teckas start at $1,400 per month, all-in. Per BLS, the US median wage for graphic designers is roughly $58,910 per year. Agency-level mid designers run $6,000 to $8,500 per month fully loaded in major US cities.
Do designers handle motion and video?
Many do light motion in After Effects or Canva for social reels and short ad creative. For heavier motion or video editing, we place a dedicated motion designer or video editor as a separate seat. Book a call to scope the mix.
