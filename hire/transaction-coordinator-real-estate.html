What a Real Estate Transaction Coordinator Does for Your Business

A real estate transaction coordinator remote hire is the person who takes a signed contract across the finish line. From the moment a purchase agreement is executed, your TC opens the file, tracks every contingency and deadline, herds signatures, coordinates with title, lender, co-op agents, and inspectors, and makes sure nothing slips through the cracks before closing. For a top-producing agent or small team, a dedicated TC is the difference between closing 30 deals a year and 80.

Teams that outsource a remote TC for real estate typically recover 10 to 15 hours per agent per week. That time goes straight back into prospecting, showings, and listing appointments, which is where commissions actually get made. Our transaction coordinator outsourcing model gives you a full-time dedicated TC for less than the cost of a part-time local hire.

Key Responsibilities of a Remote Real Estate TC

  • Open new transactions in Dotloop, SkySlope, or Brokermint within 24 hours of executed contract
  • Build and maintain the master timeline: inspection, appraisal, loan, title, closing
  • Send introduction emails to buyer, seller, co-op agent, lender, and title company
  • Chase missing signatures, disclosures, and addenda via DocuSign or Dotloop
  • Schedule inspections, appraisals, and final walkthroughs
  • Monitor contingency deadlines and send reminders 48 to 72 hours in advance
  • Coordinate with title, escrow, and the lender on clear-to-close
  • Prepare the broker compliance file and submit for review
  • Send closing gift requests, review forms, and past-client nurture kickoff
  • Update the CRM (Follow Up Boss, kvCORE, BoomTown) with file status
  • Run weekly pipeline reports for the agent or team lead

Why Real Estate Teams Outsource Transaction Coordination

Most agents do not leave the business because they cannot sell. They burn out on paperwork. The average residential transaction generates 40 to 80 documents and requires coordination across 6 to 10 outside parties. Missing a single deadline can tank a deal or trigger a broker compliance issue.

Outsourcing a remote TC for real estate solves three specific pain points: capacity during peak season, compliance consistency across agents, and cost. Frequently Asked Questions

What does a real estate transaction coordinator do?
A real estate transaction coordinator manages every step from executed contract to closing. That includes opening escrow, tracking contingency and inspection deadlines, collecting compliance documents, coordinating with title, lender, co-op agents, and inspectors, and preparing the file for broker review. Our remote TCs handle 20 to 40 active files per agent depending on volume.
Which transaction management software do your TCs use?
Our transaction coordinators work daily in Dotloop, SkySlope, DocuSign Rooms, Brokermint, Paperless Pipeline, and Trello. They also know Follow Up Boss, kvCORE, and common MLS systems. We train on your specific stack during onboarding at no cost.
How much does a remote transaction coordinator cost?
Full-time remote TCs through Teckas start at $1,400 per month, all-in. Local US TCs typically run $3,500 to $5,500 per month or $300 to $500 per file through contract-only TC services. Most teams closing 4 plus deals per month save money by moving to a dedicated remote TC.
Can a remote TC sign documents or represent our brokerage?
No. A transaction coordinator supports the licensed agent and broker of record. They prepare, organize, and route documents, chase signatures, and keep files compliant, but all licensed activity is performed by your agents. This keeps your brokerage inside state real estate law.
