What a Remote Tax Prep Assistant Does for Your Accounting Firm

A remote tax prep assistant for accounting firms is the production layer that turns a stack of client source documents into a review-ready draft return. They organize 1040 and 1120/1120-S/1065 client organizers, enter W-2s, 1099s, K-1s, and Schedule C data into Drake, UltraTax, or Lacerte, prepare depreciation and fixed asset entries, reconcile prior-year carryforwards, and route the return to the signing CPA or EA for review. All substantive tax positions, planning, and signature stay with the licensed professional, consistent with IRS Circular 230.

Teckas places full-time dedicated tax prep assistants trained on US individual and business returns. During busy season, many firms double or triple their effective production capacity by pairing each US CPA with 1 to 3 remote preparers. That is the difference between declining new clients in February and absorbing them.

Key Responsibilities of a Remote Tax Prep Assistant

  • Organize and index client documents in SmartVault, TaxDome, or Canopy
  • Chase missing documents via secure portal messages
  • Prepare draft 1040s including Schedule A, B, C, D, E, and SE
  • Prepare draft 1065, 1120, 1120-S, and 990 returns for CPA review
  • Data entry for W-2, 1099, K-1, 1098, 5498, and brokerage 1099-B
  • Fixed asset and depreciation schedules in the tax software
  • Prior-year rollover review and carryforward reconciliation
  • State return preparation for multi-state filers
  • Sales and use tax return prep support for business clients
  • E-file package assembly for CPA review and signature
  • Post-season: extensions, amended returns, IRS notice response drafting

Why Accounting Firms Outsource Tax Prep

Tax season capacity is the central problem of every accounting firm. The AICPA PCPS pipeline report has shown a shrinking supply of US CPAs for a decade, and junior-level tax preparers are in short supply across every metro. Seasonal preparers cost $40 to $75 per hour during Q1. Hiring full-time staff for a 14-week peak destroys margin for the other 38 weeks.

A dedicated offshore tax prep assistant solves both sides. You get year-round capacity at roughly a quarter of the loaded cost of US staff, so busy season is covered and the rest of the year gets used for cleanup, write-ups, planning, and advisory work. Outsourcing the preparation layer under CPA review is expressly contemplated by IRS Circular 230 and AICPA Statement on Standards for Tax Services, provided the licensed preparer remains responsible for the return.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a remote tax prep assistant do?
A remote tax prep assistant supports CPAs and EAs by organizing client documents, entering return data into tax software, preparing draft individual and business returns for CPA review, handling client document requests, and managing e-file follow-up. All returns are reviewed and signed by a licensed CPA or EA.
Can a remote tax prep assistant sign or file returns?
No. Our assistants prepare draft returns under the direct supervision of a licensed US CPA or EA. The signing, review, and e-file submission remains with the licensed professional. This is consistent with IRS Circular 230 and AICPA guidance on the use of nonlicensed staff in tax preparation.
