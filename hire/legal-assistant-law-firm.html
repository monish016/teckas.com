What a Remote Legal Assistant Does for Your Law Firm

A remote legal assistant for a law firm handles the administrative layer that keeps a practice running: new client intake, scheduling, calendar and deadline management, document preparation and formatting, billing entry, and routine client communication. Every task is performed under the direct supervision of a licensed US attorney, consistent with ABA Model Rule 5.3 on the responsibility for nonlawyer assistance. Our legal assistants do not practice law, do not provide legal advice, and do not sign pleadings.

For solo attorneys and small firms, a virtual legal assistant is often the first hire that pays for itself within 60 days. According to Clio's Legal Trends Report, attorneys only bill 2.5 of every 8 hours on average. The rest is lost to admin. A dedicated remote legal assistant recovers 15 to 25 billable hours per attorney per week.

Key Responsibilities of a Remote Legal Assistant

  • New client intake: conflict checks, engagement letters, retainer collection
  • Calendar management and court date tracking (under attorney review)
  • Document preparation and formatting from attorney templates
  • E-filing preparation and docket tracking support
  • Time entry and billing capture in Clio, MyCase, or PracticePanther
  • Invoice generation, trust accounting entries, and LawPay processing
  • Client communication on scheduling, status updates, and document requests
  • Medical records requests for PI and disability files
  • Deposition and hearing scheduling with court reporters and opposing counsel
  • File organization, indexing, and Bates labeling support
  • Deadline docketing into case management and rule-based reminders

Why Law Firms Outsource to a Virtual Legal Assistant

Solo attorneys and small firms face a capacity problem: the work needed to serve 50 active matters is admin-heavy, but hiring a full-time in-office assistant at $50,000 to $65,000 per year plus benefits does not pencil out until you have 3 attorneys. A virtual legal assistant service gives you the leverage without the overhead.

Outsource law firm admin arrangements are increasingly common and expressly contemplated by state bar guidance. ABA Formal Opinion 498 on virtual practice, and various state bar ethics opinions, permit the use of offshore nonlawyer assistants provided the supervising attorney ensures competence, confidentiality, and compliance with the rules of professional conduct. Frequently Asked Questions

What does a remote legal assistant do?
A remote legal assistant supports licensed attorneys with client intake, scheduling, calendar management, document preparation and formatting, billing entry in practice management software, and routine client communication. All substantive legal work remains under direct attorney supervision.
Can a remote legal assistant give legal advice?
No. Our remote legal assistants do not provide legal advice, make legal judgments, or engage in the practice of law. They perform administrative and clerical support functions under the direct supervision of a licensed US attorney, consistent with ABA Model Rule 5.3 on nonlawyer assistance.
Which practice management software do your legal assistants know?
Our remote legal assistants have daily experience with Clio Manage, Clio Grow, MyCase, PracticePanther, Smokeball, CosmoLex, Filevine, and Litify. They also know LawPay, QuickBooks, and DocuSign for common billing and execution workflows.
