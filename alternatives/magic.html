Magic Assistant Alternative: Dedicated Remote Staffing

Weighing Magic's on-demand assistant model against a dedicated full-time team member? Here is a factual, side-by-side look at how Teckas compares, and which model fits which kind of work.

If you are searching for a Magic assistant alternative (also known as Get Magic), you are likely a business that has used Magic for on-demand task support and is weighing whether a dedicated full-time assistant would be a better fit, or you are evaluating both and comparing models. Magic and Teckas are not direct competitors in the strictest sense; they are built for different problems. This page explains which model solves which problem honestly.

What Is Magic?

Magic, operating at getmagic.com, is an on-demand, task-based assistant service. Based on their public positioning, clients send tasks through chat or another interface and the task is handled by an assistant from Magic's pool, which means the specific person handling a given task can vary. Magic publicly emphasizes AI-assisted workflows as part of their service delivery. Pricing is structured on an hourly or subscription basis, which is well suited to variable, on-demand usage.

What Is Teckas?

Teckas is a managed remote staffing firm that places dedicated full-time professionals from India with US and Canadian businesses. Every placement is a single specific person who works 40 hours a week exclusively for one client. Pricing is a flat all-inclusive monthly rate from $1,200 to $2,500. Teckas staff work full US or Canadian shifts, and we handle employment, HR, payroll, and retention.

Why People Search for Magic Alternatives

The most common reason we hear is continuity. Magic's pool-based model is efficient for sporadic tasks but can mean different assistants handle different tasks, which works well for isolated requests and less well when a role needs to build context. Buyers who realize their needs have grown from occasional tasks to consistent, context-heavy work often start looking for a dedicated option.

A second reason is cost predictability at volume. On-demand pricing is excellent when usage is variable, but once you find you are regularly using 20 to 40 hours a week of assistant support, a flat monthly rate for a dedicated person usually comes out cheaper and more predictable.

What Magic Does Well

  • True on-demand: Send a task, get it handled, no need to fill a workday. The pool-based model makes this possible.
  • Variable workload fit: If your need fluctuates week to week, Magic's pricing scales down during quiet periods in a way a full-time hire cannot.
  • AI-assisted workflows: Magic publicly integrates AI into their delivery, which can speed up certain task types.
  • Fast activation: On-demand services typically onboard fast because no specific-person matching is required.
  • Breadth of task types: The pool model means a variety of skills are available across different assistants.

What Teckas Does Differently

  • One dedicated person: Same professional every day, working 40 hours a week. Context, systems, and preferences stay with that one hire.
  • Flat monthly pricing: $1,200 to $2,500 per month all-inclusive. No hour counting, no variable billing, no overage surprises.
  • Deep role coverage: Beyond VA tasks, Teckas staffs developers, finance professionals, analysts, ops managers, and specialists.
  • Managed employment: We handle HR, payroll, benefits, and performance support. It is a staffing relationship, not a vendor-managed task queue.
  • Full time zone shift: Your Teckas pro is online during your full business day, every day, not task-by-task.

When Magic Is the Better Choice

Magic is clearly the better fit when:

  • Your needs are sporadic and variable. If you use an assistant five hours one week and twenty the next, an on-demand model scales naturally in a way a full-time hire cannot.
  • Your tasks are discrete and do not require deep context-building with one specific person
  • You want the ability to send a task and walk away, without managing a person
  • You value AI-assisted task workflows as part of the delivery
  • You are experimenting with delegation and want a low-commitment way to test

We are straightforward here: if your needs genuinely are sporadic and task-based, Magic's model is elegant for that problem. Do not overpay for a full-time person you will not use.

When Teckas Is the Better Fit

Teckas tends to be the better fit when:

  • You need 20+ hours a week of consistent support
  • You want the same person every day so context and working relationship compound
  • Your role extends beyond admin tasks into finance, development, analytics, operations, or specialized work
  • You want flat predictable pricing, not hour counting
  • You want a managed staffing partner, not a task marketplace

On-demand or dedicated? The answer is usually obvious after one conversation.

Making the Decision

A simple filter: if you can describe your need as a list of discrete tasks that would not benefit much from one person building knowledge over time, Magic's model fits. If your need is really a role that wants an employee's brain applied to it (context, ownership, follow-through), a dedicated hire fits better. Some clients use both: Magic for overflow bursts, Teckas for the consistent operational role.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Teckas cheaper than Magic?
They price differently. Magic publicly offers hourly or subscription pricing for on-demand task support. Teckas prices a flat $1,200 to $2,500 per month all-inclusive for a dedicated full-time professional. For sporadic, low-hour needs, Magic's on-demand model can be less expensive. For consistent full-time work, Teckas is often lower on a per-hour basis and adds continuity of one consistent person.
What is the main difference between Magic and Teckas?
Magic operates a pool-based, on-demand assistant service where tasks can be routed to different assistants with AI-assisted workflows, per their public positioning. Teckas assigns a single dedicated professional who works full-time for one client only. Magic is built for variable, sporadic task needs; Teckas is built for consistent full-time roles.
Do I get the same assistant every time with Teckas?
Yes. Every Teckas placement is a single dedicated professional working 40 hours per week for you. Same person, every day. Context, systems, and preferences build up with that one hire rather than being re-explained each task.
Can I use Magic for occasional tasks and Teckas for my main role?
Yes, and some clients do exactly that. Magic's on-demand model handles sporadic overflow well, while a dedicated Teckas professional handles consistent operational work. They are not mutually exclusive.
