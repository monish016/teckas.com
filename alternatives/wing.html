Wing Assistant (wingassistant.com) has become a popular name in the virtual assistant category, especially for startups and solopreneurs. If you are looking at Wing as a Wing Assistant alternative or searching for alternatives to Wing, this page breaks down the real differences between Wing and Teckas so you can pick the right fit. What Is Wing Assistant?

Wing Assistant is a US-headquartered virtual assistant company that sources talent primarily from the Philippines and Latin America. Based on their publicly available website, Wing offers tiered monthly plans that, at the time of this writing, range from approximately $599 per month on entry plans up to roughly $2,400 per month on higher tiers. Lower tiers often involve part-time hours or shared/pool-based assistant arrangements, while higher tiers move toward dedicated full-time support. Wing is well known for being accessible to startups, founders, and solopreneurs who want to get started fast without a long commitment.

What Is Teckas?

Teckas is a managed remote staffing firm that places India-based professionals as dedicated full-time team members with US and Canadian businesses. Our pricing is flat: $1,200 to $2,500 per month all-inclusive for a dedicated professional working 40 hours per week. There are no part-time tiers, no shared VA pools, and no hourly usage caps. Every Teckas placement is one person, working for one client, with full HR, payroll, benefits, and management support included.

Why People Look for Wing Alternatives

Common reasons we see buyers comparing Wing vs Teckas:

  • Wanting a single named dedicated professional rather than a pool or shared arrangement
  • Needing more hours than Wing's entry tiers provide
  • Preferring India-based talent, especially for technical, finance, or analytical roles
  • Looking for roles beyond general VA work
  • Wanting deeper integration with the client's workflows and tooling

What Wing Does Well

Wing has real strengths and is a legitimate choice for the right buyer:

  • Accessible entry pricing: The starter tiers are affordable and well-suited to founders who want VA support without a big commitment.
  • Fast onboarding: Their model is designed for quick setup, which matters for solopreneurs who need help today.
  • Broad popularity with startups: They have a large, visible brand in the solopreneur and startup community.
  • Tier flexibility: You can start small and scale up within their plan structure.
  • Philippines and LatAm talent: Strong for consumer-facing tasks and US time zone overlap from LatAm.

Dedicated vs Pool-Based: What It Means in Practice

This is the core difference worth understanding. In a pool-based or shared model, multiple clients share a VA's capacity, or an assistant is allocated to tasks rather than to a client. That works for predictable, process-driven tasks: calendar management, email triage, data entry. Where it gets thinner is when you need someone who deeply understands your workflow, remembers your customers, builds trust with your internal systems, and makes decisions inside your playbook. That requires a dedicated person over time.

Teckas only offers dedicated. That is a feature for some buyers and overkill for others. If you have four hours a week of lightweight VA tasks, Wing's entry tier is more practical. If you need a named person who shows up every day, learns your business, and grows into more scope, dedicated is the right model.

When Wing Is the Better Choice

Wing is likely the better choice when:

  • You are a solopreneur with genuinely light VA needs (under 20 hours per week)
  • You are not ready to commit to a full-time hire
  • You prefer Philippines or LatAm talent
  • You want the lowest entry price point in the managed VA category
  • Your tasks are well-suited to a pool or shared model

When Teckas Is the Better Fit

Teckas tends to be the better fit when:

  • You need a dedicated person who works 40 hours a week exclusively for you
  • You want India-based talent, especially for technical, financial, or analytical roles
  • Your tasks require deep workflow knowledge and system access
  • You want one flat predictable price without tier upgrades
  • You are scaling from "I need help" to "I need a team member"
  • You are a Canadian business looking for a provider explicitly built for your jurisdiction

A Note on Scaling

Many clients who start with Wing or similar part-time VA services eventually graduate to dedicated staffing as their needs grow. The signs: you find yourself wishing your VA was available more hours, you want them to own an entire function rather than tasks, or you are looking for a specialist (developer, bookkeeper, analyst) rather than a general VA. That is usually the moment Teckas becomes the better structural fit.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Wing Assistant and Teckas?
Wing Assistant offers tiered VA plans with pricing publicly listed from around $599 per month up to roughly $2,400 per month, with talent from the Philippines and Latin America. Lower tiers may involve pool or shared assistant arrangements. Teckas provides only dedicated full-time professionals from India, working 40 hours a week for one client at a flat $1,200 to $2,500 per month all-inclusive. The models target different needs: Wing for lighter-touch part-time VA support, Teckas for dedicated full-time hires.
Does Teckas offer part-time or shared VAs?
No. Teckas exclusively staffs full-time dedicated professionals at 40 hours per week. If your need is genuinely part-time or you prefer a pool-based VA model for occasional tasks, Wing's lower tiers may fit better. If you need a dedicated team member who integrates deeply with your workflows, Teckas is designed for that.
Is Teckas more expensive than Wing?
On a per-month dollar basis at the lowest tiers, Wing's entry plans are cheaper. On a full-time equivalent basis, the comparison flips because those entry tiers cover fewer hours or pool-based time. For 40-hour dedicated support, the two are similar in total cost; Teckas at $1,200 to $2,500 per month is competitive with Wing's higher tiers for comparable full-time work.
Which is better for a solopreneur?
If you are a solopreneur with genuinely light, occasional VA needs, Wing's entry tier may be more practical than hiring a dedicated full-time person. If you are a solopreneur or small business ready to commit to a full-time dedicated hire who will deeply learn your business, Teckas is the better structure.
