Why businesses hire virtual assistants

Virtual assistants have become a standard part of how growing businesses scale. A founder or executive typically spends a large share of their week on scheduling, inbox triage, travel, research, basic reporting, and follow-ups. A competent VA handles most of that in a fraction of the time and at a small fraction of the cost of a full-time local hire. The global VA industry has grown steadily for a decade, and the options have expanded from hourly freelancers to fully managed dedicated staffing firms. The trade-off buyers now make is not whether to hire a VA but which model, region, and price point fits their work.

How we chose the services on this list

We looked for services that are established, have transparent pricing signals on their public websites, and have a clear business model. We avoided single-freelancer platforms because those are closer to marketplaces than services. For each entry we list the model, pricing range, talent region, best-for segment, and an honest limitation.

  1. Established reputation. Operating for more than three years with a verifiable business presence.
  2. Transparent positioning. Clear information on what they do and roughly what they charge.
  3. Dedicated or near-dedicated model. Strong preference for services that assign a specific person rather than pure pool labor.
  4. North American client focus. Services that commonly serve US and Canadian businesses.

1. Teckas

Model. Managed remote staffing that places dedicated full-time professionals from India with US and Canadian companies. Every Teckas hire works 40 hours per week exclusively for one client, with HR, payroll, benefits, and equipment allowance included.

Pricing. $1,200 to $2,500 per month all-inclusive. Month-to-month, no minimum term.

Region. India.

Best for. US and Canadian businesses hiring a dedicated executive assistant, operations coordinator, finance analyst, or back-office professional as a long-term team member.

Honest limitation. Teckas is not a task-based subscription. If you only need five hours a week or sporadic tasks, a subscription like Wing or a task service like Magic is a cleaner fit.

2. Wing Assistant

Model. Tiered subscription VA service with a polished onboarding platform. Part-time and full-time plans available.

Pricing. Publicly listed at approximately $599 to $2,400 per month depending on tier and hours.

Region. Philippines and LatAm.

Best for. Startups and solopreneurs who want tiered pricing, friendly software, and a broad general VA catalog.

Honest limitation. Entry tiers are part-time and the model leans toward general VA tasks. Specialized roles like development, finance, or paid media are outside Wing's core catalog.

3. Time Etc

Model. Hourly-block virtual assistant service with UK and US-based VAs. You pre-purchase hours and draw against them as needed.

Pricing. Publicly listed from approximately $29 per hour and up depending on block size.

Region. UK and US.

Best for. Executives and professionals who want native English VAs for part-time, flexible tasks and prefer an hourly block model.

Honest limitation. Hourly blocks create overhead for full-time work and the price per hour is much higher than offshore alternatives. Not the right fit for 40-hour-a-week roles.

4. BELAY

Model. US-based contractor matching service across executive assistants, bookkeepers, social media managers, and website specialists. High-touch matching.

Pricing. Premium, quoted after a discovery call rather than publicly listed.

Region. United States.

Best for. Executives and small business owners who specifically want US-based contractors and are willing to pay a premium.

Honest limitation. Cost is significantly higher than offshore alternatives. If budget is the primary driver, other options on this list will be materially cheaper.

5. Magic

Model. On-demand task and dedicated assistant service. Originally known for SMS and chat-based task handling, now with dedicated VA options.

Pricing. Publicly listed at approximately $10 per hour and up for dedicated plans.

Region. Philippines.

Best for. Businesses that want task-based on-demand support with the option to transition to a dedicated assistant.

Honest limitation. The task-pool origins mean continuity can vary compared to a fully dedicated managed hire, though their dedicated plans address this.

6. Virtual Latinos

Model. Direct-hire facilitation service that sources LatAm candidates and helps you employ them directly. Charges a placement fee rather than ongoing managed staffing.

Pricing. A placement fee plus the monthly salary you pay the contractor. Typically publicly listed as flat placement pricing.

Region. LatAm (Mexico, Colombia, Argentina and others).

Best for. US businesses that want LatAm time zone overlap and prefer a direct employment relationship rather than a managed staffing layer.

Honest limitation. Once hired, you are responsible for payment, HR, and retention. No ongoing managed services unless separately contracted.

7. Prialto

Model. Enterprise-grade managed assistant service with a strong operational support team behind each placement. Focuses on executives and sales teams.

Pricing. Premium, publicly listed starting at approximately $1,700 per month and up.

Region. Philippines, Kenya, and Guatemala.

Best for. Enterprise executives and large sales teams who want a heavy support layer and formal processes.

Honest limitation. The enterprise model and operational overhead make it more expensive than most offshore VA options. Not the right fit for individual founders or lean startups.

How to pick the right virtual assistant service

Use these heuristics to narrow the list based on your actual need.

  • If you need a full-time dedicated professional. Teckas or Prialto. Teckas for India talent at mid-market pricing; Prialto for enterprise scale.
  • If you need flexible tiered VA hours. Wing Assistant. Clean subscriptions, multiple regions.
  • If you need native-English part-time VAs. Time Etc. Hourly blocks with UK and US talent.
  • If you only want US-based talent. BELAY. Higher cost, but US contractors.
  • If you need LatAm time zone coverage. Virtual Latinos for direct hire, or Wing for a managed LatAm VA.
  • If your work is unpredictable tasks rather than ongoing role. Magic. Task-pool design is built for it.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best virtual assistant service in 2026?
The best virtual assistant service depends on your use case. For dedicated full-time professionals from India with managed HR, Teckas is a strong fit. For flexible tiered subscriptions, Wing Assistant is popular. For hourly blocks, Time Etc is well regarded. For US-based VAs, BELAY is premium. There is no single winner across every scenario.
How much does a virtual assistant service cost?
Offshore virtual assistant services typically cost between $500 and $2,500 per month for dedicated or near-dedicated hours. US-based services cost significantly more, often $3,500 and up per month. Hourly pricing starts at approximately $4 to $10 per hour offshore and $25 to $60 per hour for US-based contractors.
Is a dedicated VA better than a pool VA?
For long-term full-time work, a dedicated VA is almost always better because they learn your systems, people, and preferences. For short, transactional tasks, a pool VA can be more cost-efficient. Most serious businesses eventually move to dedicated.
Can a VA handle specialized tasks like bookkeeping or development?
Yes, though the service matters. General VA subscriptions typically handle admin, email, scheduling, and basic research. For specialized work like bookkeeping, paid media management, or software development, you usually need a provider that recruits for those roles specifically, such as Teckas for dedicated professional roles or BELAY for US-based bookkeepers.
How fast can I hire a virtual assistant?
Subscription VA services can start within a few days. Dedicated managed hires usually take two to four weeks from scoping to start because the provider recruits a specific match for your role rather than pulling from a pool.
