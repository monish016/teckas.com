Direct Answer: What an Offshore Developer Costs

A mid-level full-stack developer typically costs $95,000 to $135,000 per year in base salary in the US (BLS Occupational Employment Statistics and Levels.fyi 2025-2026 data), or $150,000 to $200,000 fully loaded with benefits and overhead. Through managed remote staffing from India, the same role costs $2,000 to $2,500 per month all-inclusive - $24,000 to $30,000 per year fully loaded. Eastern Europe runs $60,000 to $90,000 and Latin America runs $60,000 to $100,000 fully loaded.

Offshore software developer rates are the most scrutinized number in any engineering budget because engineering payroll is the single largest line item at most software companies. This guide uses BLS, Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, Stack Overflow Developer Survey, and Naukri data to show real 2026 cost ranges by region, seniority, and specialty, plus the hidden TCO factors that rarely show up in a job listing.

Hourly vs Full-Time Pricing for Developers

Hourly ($50 to $180 per hour for US freelancers, $15 to $50 offshore). Best for discrete projects under 3 months, spec-driven work, or emergency capacity. Marketplaces like Toptal, Arc, and Upwork dominate this segment. Trade-off: you pay a premium per hour and lose institutional knowledge the moment the project closes.

Dedicated full-time ($2,000 to $2,500 per month from India, $12,500 to $17,000 in the US). The right model for any product team. Cheaper per hour, compounds context, and enables real ownership of production systems. This is where every growth-stage startup converges.

Crossover math: at 40 hours per week for 12 months, a $75 per hour US freelancer costs about $156,000. A dedicated Teckas mid-level developer at $2,250 per month costs $27,000 over the same period - and you have them for the next hire on day 1.

What Drives Developer Cost

Seniority. The biggest single lever. US senior-to-junior spread is roughly 2.5x; India spread is roughly 5x. Managed partners absorb that variance into a narrower band for clients.

Specialty. AI/ML, data engineering, and SRE roles price 25 to 50 percent above generalist full-stack in every region. Mobile (iOS, Android, React Native) sits 10 to 20 percent above generalist web.

Stack. Go, Rust, Elixir, and specialized data platforms (Snowflake, Databricks) carry premiums. JavaScript, Python, and Java generalists are the cheapest per unit of skill.

Commitment. 12-month contracts price 15 to 25 percent below month-to-month in every region because the provider amortizes recruitment cost across a longer window.

Hidden Costs in Local Hiring

  • Benefits overhead (30-40%). Health, 401(k) match, FICA, FUTA, PTO, equity dilution - all real TCO line items.
  • Recruitment (20-25% of first-year salary). Tech recruiters charge 20 to 25 percent for mid to senior engineering roles, and time-to-fill averages 42 to 60 days (LinkedIn Talent Insights).
  • Turnover. SHRM data puts replacement cost for a mid-level engineer at 50 to 150 percent of annual salary. US engineer tenure averages 1.8 to 2.4 years in tech companies.
  • Equipment and software. $3,000 to $6,000 per year per engineer for machine, IDE licenses, cloud sandbox, and observability tools.
  • Opportunity cost of delay. Each extra week of hiring time is a week of product velocity lost. A 42-day US hire vs a 7-day Teckas placement is 5 extra weeks of shipping.

Common Questions

How much does an offshore developer cost?
A mid-level full-stack developer in the US costs $95,000 to $135,000 in base salary per BLS and Levels.fyi, $150,000 to $200,000 fully loaded. The same role from India through a managed partner costs $2,000 to $2,500 per month, or $24,000 to $30,000 per year fully loaded. Eastern Europe runs $60,000 to $90,000 and Latin America runs $60,000 to $100,000 fully loaded.
What is the total cost of ownership for a developer in India vs the US?
US TCO: base salary + 30 to 40 percent benefits + recruitment fees (20 to 25 percent of first-year salary) + equipment + turnover risk. India managed TCO via Teckas is a single monthly fee with recruitment, HR, IT, payroll, and replacement guarantee bundled in. A $120,000 US mid-level role typically costs $160,000 to $185,000 fully loaded vs $24,000 to $30,000 in India managed.
Are Indian developers cheaper than Eastern European developers?
Yes. India managed developers at $24,000 to $36,000 per year fully loaded are 40 to 60 percent cheaper than Eastern European equivalents at $60,000 to $90,000 per year. Eastern Europe offers closer timezones for European buyers but India offers deeper talent pools and lower TCO.
What are the hidden costs of hiring a developer in the US?
Hidden costs include health insurance ($12,000 to $24,000 for a senior hire with dependents), 401(k) match, equity dilution, payroll taxes, equipment and software ($3,000 to $6,000 per year), recruiter fees, and turnover replacement cost (SHRM estimates 50 to 150 percent of salary). These add 30 to 40 percent on top of base, plus one-off recruitment of 20 to 25 percent of first-year salary.
Can offshore developers work US time zones?
Yes. Teckas developers provide 4 to 5 hours of overlap with US business hours by default and can work full US hours on request. Most teams find 4 hours sufficient for standups, pairing, code review, and live troubleshooting.
