Direct Answer: What a Virtual Assistant Costs

A virtual assistant typically costs $42,000 to $55,000 per year in the US, or $25 to $50 per hour. Through managed remote staffing from India, the same role costs $1,200 to $1,500 per month all-inclusive - roughly $7.50 to $9.40 per hour equivalent at a 160-hour month. Philippines marketplace VAs run $5 to $12 per hour, and Eastern European VAs run $15 to $30 per hour.

Those numbers look simple on a line chart. In practice, what you actually pay depends on scope (admin vs specialized), commitment (hourly vs full-time), and whether you carry benefits, payroll, and turnover risk yourself or buy them bundled through a managed provider. This guide breaks down the virtual assistant price question across every real-world hiring path, using published data from the US Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), Glassdoor, Indeed, Statistics Canada, and Naukri.

Hourly vs Full-Time Pricing: When Each Makes Sense

The VA hourly rate 2026 market splits cleanly into two modes, and picking the wrong mode is where most buyers overspend.

Hourly makes sense when: your workload is under 15 hours per week, highly variable, or you need a niche specialty (podcast editing, paid ads reporting, legal intake) for a single project. Marketplaces like Upwork and Fiverr are efficient for this. Expect to pay $25 to $60 per hour for US-based specialists and $8 to $20 per hour for offshore specialists.

Full-time (or dedicated part-time) makes sense when: your workload is above 20 hours per week, consistent, and requires institutional knowledge (your tools, your customers, your voice). Above that threshold, full-time is always cheaper per hour and dramatically better on quality because the VA is not constantly re-onboarding. Most businesses that start hourly move to a dedicated model within 6 months.

At 40 hours per week for 12 months, a $35 per hour US VA costs roughly $72,800. A dedicated Teckas VA at $1,350 per month costs $16,200 for the same period. That is the difference between a one-person back office and a one-person back office plus two more hires you could not previously afford.

What Drives the Cost of a Virtual Assistant

Four factors determine where a specific VA lands inside the ranges above.

Experience. A VA with 1 to 2 years of generalist experience sits at the bottom of the range. A VA with 5-plus years plus a specialty (executive support, bookkeeping, CRM admin) sits 40 to 70 percent higher.

Specialty. Generalist admin is the cheapest tier. Add a vertical skill (real estate transaction coordination, medical intake, paralegal support, Shopify management) and expected rates rise 25 to 50 percent in every geography.

Location. Even within a country, location matters. A US VA in a low-cost-of-living state charges less than one in New York or San Francisco. In India, Tier 1 cities (Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi) price 15 to 25 percent above Tier 2 and 3 cities.

Commitment. A 40-hour weekly commitment buys a 20 to 30 percent lower blended rate than ad-hoc hourly work, because the VA or provider amortizes vetting, training, and idle-time risk across a guaranteed block.

Hidden Costs in Local Hiring

The sticker price of a US VA understates true cost by 30 to 40 percent. Here is what most businesses miss:

  • Benefits overhead (30-40%). Health insurance ($8,000 to $18,000 per year per employee), 401(k) match (3 to 6 percent), payroll taxes (7.65 percent FICA plus FUTA), paid time off, and equipment.
  • Recruitment. Contingent search fees run 15 to 25 percent of first-year salary for admin roles. In-house recruiting costs time that pulls from revenue work.
  • Turnover. SHRM puts the replacement cost of a non-technical hire at 50 to 75 percent of annual salary when you include lost productivity and re-training. VA roles turn over at roughly 30 percent annually in the US.
  • Management time. Unmanaged hires need an hour or more per week of direct supervision from a leader whose time is worth 5 to 10x the VA's rate.
  • Software and equipment. Laptop, productivity suite, comms tools, security stack - typically $1,500 to $3,500 per seat per year.

A managed remote staffing model absorbs most of this into a single monthly rate. That is why a $1,350 per month India VA is not just 70 percent cheaper than a US VA at the base line - it is closer to 80 percent cheaper on true TCO.

What to Budget in 2026

For planning a first VA hire in 2026, here are the budgets that actually work in practice, by business stage:

  • Solo founder / side business: $400 to $900 per month for 10 to 20 hours per week offshore, or $600 to $1,500 on hourly marketplaces.
  • Small business (5-25 employees): $1,200 to $1,800 per month for a full-time dedicated managed VA from India - this is the sweet spot.
  • Mid-market (25-200 employees): $3,500 to $9,000 per month for a team of 3 to 6 VAs across executive support, bookkeeping, and customer ops.
  • Agencies and professional services: $1,500 to $2,500 per month per VA for specialized roles (paralegal intake, real estate TC, account coordination).

Common Questions

How much does a virtual assistant cost per hour in 2026?
US-based virtual assistants charge $25 to $50 per hour on average, with specialized VAs reaching $75 per hour. Through managed remote staffing from India, full-time VAs work out to roughly $7.50 to $9.40 per hour equivalent when calculated from a $1,200 to $1,500 monthly rate across 160 working hours.
How much does a full-time virtual assistant cost per year?
A full-time US-based VA costs $42,000 to $55,000 per year in base salary according to BLS and Glassdoor 2025-2026 data, plus 30 to 40 percent in benefits and payroll overhead. A full-time managed VA from India through Teckas costs $14,400 to $18,000 per year fully loaded.
Is it cheaper to hire a VA from India or the Philippines?
Philippines VAs average $5 to $12 per hour through marketplaces, and India managed VAs work out to $7.50 to $9.40 per hour equivalent at full-time rates. Philippines can look cheaper at the hourly line but India typically wins on retention, English writing, and specialized tasks like bookkeeping support, research, and executive coordination.
What are the hidden costs of hiring a local VA?
Hidden costs include payroll taxes (around 7.65 percent FICA/FUTA), health insurance ($8,000 to $18,000 per year), paid time off, equipment, software licenses, recruitment fees (often 15 to 20 percent of first-year salary), and turnover replacement cost. These add 30 to 40 percent on top of the visible hourly or salary rate.
Should I hire a VA hourly or full-time?
Hourly makes sense for under 15 hours per week of work or highly specialized one-off projects. Full-time is more cost-effective above 20 hours per week, provides consistency, and avoids constant re-onboarding. Most businesses that start hourly move to full-time within 6 months once the workload stabilizes.
