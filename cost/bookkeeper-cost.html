Direct Answer: What a Bookkeeper Costs

A bookkeeper typically costs $40,000 to $65,000 per year in the US, with the BLS reporting a median wage of $47,440. Through managed remote staffing from India, the same role costs $1,400 to $1,800 per month all-inclusive - roughly $16,800 to $21,600 per year fully loaded. Hourly rates run $30 to $60 in the US, $20 to $40 in Eastern Europe, and $8.75 to $11.25 equivalent from India at full-time dedicated rates.

Bookkeeping is one of the easiest functions to move offshore or remote because every meaningful tool - QuickBooks Online, Xero, NetSuite, Bill.com, Ramp, Brex, Gusto, ADP - is already cloud-native. The only variable is whether you pay a US payroll stack or a managed remote stack for the same work. This guide walks through the real bookkeeping services cost picture using BLS, Glassdoor, Indeed, and Statistics Canada data.

Hourly vs Monthly Retainer vs Full-Time

Bookkeeping is priced three ways, and the right model depends on transaction volume.

Hourly ($30 to $60 US, $9 to $12 offshore). Best for under 30 transactions per month, seasonal cleanup, or catchup work. Flexible but expensive per unit of output once volume grows.

Monthly retainer / bookkeeping service ($250 to $1,500 per month). Services like Bench, Pilot, and Bookkeeper360 price by transaction count. Good for 30 to 200 transactions per month, low payroll complexity. Trade-off: you share a bookkeeper with other clients and lose context when they rotate.

Dedicated full-time remote bookkeeper ($1,400 to $1,800 per month from India, $4,500-plus in the US). Best once you are above 150 transactions per month, running payroll for 5-plus employees, managing multiple entities, or doing monthly close with accruals. Institutional knowledge compounds over time and becomes a real asset during audits, funding rounds, and tax prep.

What Drives Bookkeeper Cost

Transaction volume. The single biggest driver on a retainer. A 500 transaction per month business pays 3 to 5x more than a 100 transaction per month business in the same service.

Complexity. Multi-entity, multi-currency, inventory accounting, revenue recognition under ASC 606, sales tax across multiple states - each adds 20 to 50 percent to cost.

Specialty. Industry-specific bookkeeping (construction job costing, e-commerce with Shopify and Amazon, nonprofit fund accounting, property management) commands a premium.

Software stack. Bookkeepers certified on NetSuite, Sage Intacct, or QuickBooks Enterprise price 30 to 60 percent above QuickBooks Online generalists.

Hidden Costs in Local Hiring

The remote bookkeeper price you see on a salary page is the floor, not the ceiling. Real US TCO adds:

  • Benefits and payroll overhead (30-40%). Health insurance, 401(k) match, FICA, FUTA, state unemployment, paid time off.
  • Recruitment. Accounting-specific recruiters charge 15 to 20 percent of first-year salary. In-house recruiting costs 20 to 30 hours of leader time per hire.
  • Turnover. Bookkeeper turnover in the US averages 25 percent annually. Replacement cost runs 50 to 100 percent of salary per SHRM, plus an expensive transition risk on closed books.
  • Continuing education. QuickBooks, Xero, and NetSuite certifications require annual renewal and 20 to 40 hours of paid CPE time.
  • Software seats. QuickBooks Online Advanced, Bill.com, expense tools, and a secure workstation add $2,000 to $4,000 per year per seat.

Common Questions

How much does a bookkeeper cost per hour?
US bookkeepers charge $30 to $60 per hour on average, with specialized or CPA-adjacent bookkeepers at $75 to $125 per hour. Remote bookkeepers from India through a managed provider work out to roughly $8.75 to $11.25 per hour equivalent at a $1,400 to $1,800 full-time monthly rate.
How much does a full-time bookkeeper cost per year?
The BLS Occupational Employment Statistics reports a median bookkeeper wage of $47,440 per year, with a range of $40,000 to $65,000 depending on experience and location. Adding 30 to 40 percent benefits and overhead brings US fully loaded cost to $55,000 to $90,000. India managed bookkeepers through Teckas cost $16,800 to $21,600 per year fully loaded.
Bookkeeper vs bookkeeping service: which is cheaper?
Monthly bookkeeping services (Bench, Pilot, Bookkeeper360) run $250 to $1,500 per month depending on transaction volume. A dedicated full-time bookkeeper is more expensive in absolute terms but becomes cheaper per hour above 15 hours per week of work, and gives you institutional knowledge that services do not.
Can a remote bookkeeper handle my QuickBooks and Xero?
Yes. QuickBooks Online, Xero, FreshBooks, NetSuite, Sage, and Zoho Books are all cloud-based and fully accessible remotely. Most Indian bookkeepers have QuickBooks or Xero certifications and daily experience with US chart of accounts, 1099 workflows, and sales tax reconciliation.
What size business needs a full-time bookkeeper?
Rule of thumb: businesses doing 100-plus transactions per month, running payroll for 5-plus employees, or above $1M in annual revenue usually justify a dedicated bookkeeper. Below that, a monthly service or part-time arrangement is more economical.
